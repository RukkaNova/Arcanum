Solo D&D Starter is exactly what its name says: a single instruction — "Act as a Dungeon Master. Create a simple D&D-style solo adventure for a level [X] character. Include a setting, objective, and first encounter." — published by Matthew Maynor as part of a copy-paste prompt guide. There's no character sheet, no combat resolution, no rules layer of any kind. You state a level, the model invents a setting, an objective, and a first encounter, and play proceeds as freeform narration from there.

It does what a one-line prompt can do — get a session moving with zero setup — and nothing else. There's no persistence between sessions, no mechanical weight to lean on, and no world of its own; every playthrough is only as specific as whatever level and tone you feed it.

Written for ChatGPT, but it's a plain instruction that works the same way dropped into a Claude conversation. Free, paste it in and play. 2 out of 5.
