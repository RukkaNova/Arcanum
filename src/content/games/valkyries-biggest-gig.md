Valkyrie's Biggest Gig is a copy-paste prompt that turns Claude into the gamemaster of a gritty, high-stakes solo cyberpunk RPG. It opens with a short character-creation Q&A — your role, backstory, motivations, and starting traits — then drops you into a dystopia of corporate exploitation and urban decay, running the first job and reacting to your choices through combat, hacking, and social intrigue. The prompt explicitly asks for grim outcomes, permadeath, escalating nemeses, and rewards in the form of credits and cybernetic upgrades.

It runs smoothly and scales well. The world is classic cyberpunk — credits matter more than human rights — which is as cliché as the genre gets, but the prompt's real distinguishing feature is how central it makes the body. Limb loss and cybernetic augmentation come up constantly because the instructions weight them so heavily, and that pushes play toward genuinely novel transhuman characters who modify themselves to survive. The escalation works too: defeat one villain and a new, bigger one steps in, which keeps a long campaign moving even if the hand-off occasionally feels forced.

The main weakness is the economy. The reward guidance is so generous that money and upgrades get thrown at you constantly, and the game ends up too easy — it's the first thing you'll want to tune down. The prose also leans hard on describing the cityscape and lighter on the people in it, so the world can feel more painted than populated.

A smooth, customizable cyberpunk sandbox with a genuinely interesting augmentation hook. Made by **FistFulofValkyries** and available free on their [site](https://www.fistfulofvalkyries.com/2024/03/valkyries-biggest-gig-claude3s-solo-rpg.html) — paste it into Claude to play. 3 out of 5.
