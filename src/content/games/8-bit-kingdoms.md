8-Bit Kingdoms casts you as the ruler of a fantasy kingdom, chosen from a roster of starting scenarios, each with its own geography, political situation, strengths, weaknesses, and long-term challenges. Unlike most Custom GPT RPGs, it ships with reference files behind the prompt, and it shows — your royal family, heirs, neighboring realms, and the current state of diplomacy are all established before you take a single turn.

The game then runs in five-year jumps. Each turn hands you a situation and a menu of possible actions — economic development, diplomacy and alliances, warfare and territorial expansion, internal politics, succession — or lets you propose your own. Your ruler ages over time, and eventually dies or is replaced, with an heir inheriting the throne, so you end up managing a dynasty across generations rather than piloting one character. Choices from decades earlier resurface as consequences, and the game only ends when your dynasty either achieves its long-term ambition or suffers a decisive failure — complete with a reign summary and a performance score.

It's a genuinely novel structure in a store full of single-character adventures. A free Custom GPT. 4 out of 5.
