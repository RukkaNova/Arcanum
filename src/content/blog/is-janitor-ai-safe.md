"Is Janitor AI safe?" is one of the most common questions people ask before signing up, and it deserves a straight answer rather than either blind reassurance or scare stories. The short version: **Janitor AI is a legitimate platform, not malware and not a scam — but like any third-party service, using it safely means knowing where the real risks are.** Those risks aren't where most people expect; they're in API keys, third-party proxies, and lookalike sites, not in the platform itself.

This guide covers each one honestly. If you're here to actually set it up, pair this with our [complete Janitor AI setup guide](/blog/how-to-use-janitor-ai); if you want the verdict on whether it's any good, that's the [full review](/blog/janitor-ai).

## Is It Legit, or a Scam?

The official platform is legitimate and widely used by a large community. It's not a front for anything, and the core service does what it says.

The scam risk is adjacent: **lookalike and clone sites.** The Janitor AI name gets used on other domains — some are unrelated services trading on the brand, some are outright phishing pages built to harvest logins. The single most important safety habit is making sure you're on the genuine official site (`janitorai.com`) and not a copycat domain. If a site with a similar name asks for your Janitor AI password, treat it as hostile. Never reuse your password across these lookalikes.

## Your Data and Privacy

This is where an honest answer matters most. Janitor AI is a third party, so your chats are not private the way a diary is:

- **On the free built-in model,** your messages pass through Janitor AI's servers and are handled under its privacy policy.
- **With bring-your-own-model,** your messages go to *your* API provider (OpenAI, DeepSeek, and so on) instead — so the privacy terms that apply are theirs.

Either way, the safe rule is simple: **never put real personal information into roleplay chats.** No real full name, address, workplace, financial details, or anything that could identify you. Roleplay is fiction; keep your real identity out of it. That one habit neutralises most privacy concerns regardless of which model you use.

## Are API Keys and Proxies Safe? (The Real Risk)

This is the part that actually matters and the part most "is Janitor AI safe" articles skip.

**Using your own API key is fine — with an official provider.** When you connect your own model through Janitor AI's built-in bring-your-own-model setting, using a key you generated from an official provider (OpenAI, DeepSeek, OpenRouter), the setup is as safe as using that provider anywhere else.

**Third-party reverse proxies are the genuine risk.** Communities sometimes share "free" proxies — endpoints that route your requests through someone else's server. The problem is that whoever runs that server can potentially see everything you send and, depending on setup, misuse credentials. A free proxy run by a stranger is not something to trust with anything sensitive.

Practical rules that keep this safe:

- **Prefer official providers** and your own key over a stranger's proxy.
- **Set a spending limit** on your API account so a leaked or abused key can't run up a large bill.
- **Treat your API key like a password** — don't paste it into random tools, and don't share it.
- **Revoke and regenerate** any key the moment you're unsure about it; providers let you do this instantly, and it costs nothing.

Follow those and the scariest-sounding part of Janitor AI becomes a non-issue. Our [Janitor AI + DeepSeek guide](/blog/janitor-ai-deepseek) walks through connecting a budget model with your *own* key, which is the safe route.

## Account Security

Standard hygiene, because your account is the thing most worth protecting:

- **Use a strong, unique password** — not one you reuse elsewhere. If a lookalike site ever phishes it, a unique password limits the damage to that one account.
- **Be alert to phishing** — links in random messages or comments promising free perks are a classic way people get sent to fake login pages.
- **Don't install unofficial "apps" or extensions** claiming to be Janitor AI. Use the official website.

Using it safely, in the content sense, is about choosing the characters you actually want to interact with — the experience is only as mature as the cards you pick. ## Is It Safe for Your Device?

Yes — Janitor AI is a website, not software you download, so it doesn't carry malware on its own, and normal browser safety is all that's required. The device-level risks all come from *around* it: fake "Janitor AI app" downloads, sketchy proxy tools, and lookalike sites. Stay on the official website, skip unofficial downloads, and there's nothing here that threatens your computer.

## The Safe-Use Checklist

Everything above, boiled down:

- ✅ Use the **official site**; ignore lookalike domains.
- ✅ **Strong, unique password**; never reuse it.
- ✅ **No real personal information** in chats.
- ✅ Bring-your-own-model with an **official provider's key**, not a stranger's proxy.
- ✅ Set an **API spending limit**; revoke keys if unsure.
- ✅ No unofficial **apps, downloads, or extensions**.
- ✅ It's **adult-oriented** — not for minors.

Do those, and Janitor AI is about as safe as any mainstream online platform. The risks are real but ordinary, and every one of them is in your control.
