If you're choosing between **Janitor AI and Character.AI**, you're really choosing between two answers to the same question — "where do I go to talk to an AI character?" — that made very different trade-offs. Character.AI is the polished, safe, mass-market option that introduced most people to character chat in the first place. Janitor AI is the looser, more powerful option that a lot of those people migrated to when Character.AI's limits started to chafe.

Neither is simply "better." They're built for different priorities, and the right pick depends on what you actually want out of a session. This is a head-to-head on the things that decide it: the content filter, the writing quality, memory, the character library, and price.

## The Short Version

| | Janitor AI | Character.AI |
|---|---|---|
| **Core model** | In-house model **or** bring-your-own (Claude, GPT, etc.) | Own proprietary model only |
| **Content filter** | Lighter; widens further with BYOM | Strict, centralised, frequently interrupts |

| **Ease of use** | Steeper; BYOM needs setup | Very polished, beginner-friendly, strong app |

The one-line summary: **Character.AI is easier and safer; Janitor AI is freer and more powerful.** Everything below is the detail behind that sentence.

## The Filter: The Single Biggest Difference

This is the reason most "Janitor AI vs Character.AI" searches happen at all.

Character.AI runs a strict, centralised content filter, and its reputation among roleplayers is that the filter fires early and often — not only on explicit material, but on conflict, romance, violence, and morally complex scenes. A tense interrogation or a dramatic confrontation can get cut off mid-reply and replaced with a deflection, which breaks immersion exactly when a scene is working. It's a deliberate safety choice, and for younger or casual users it's arguably the right one. For storytellers who want their fiction to have real stakes, it's the dealbreaker.

Janitor AI takes the opposite stance. Its content policy is meaningfully lighter, and because it mediates prompts through a character-card layer — and lets you route through your own model — it engages with the dramatic, dark, and mature material that Character.AI refuses. If the filter is what broke Character.AI for you, Janitor AI is the direct fix. For the complete picture of no-filter options beyond these two, our [uncensored AI roleplay guide](/blog/uncensored-ai-roleplay) maps the whole landscape.

## Writing Quality: Where Bring-Your-Own-Model Changes Everything

Character.AI runs only its own proprietary model. It's tuned specifically for character chat, so it's consistent and rarely produces nonsense — but you're locked to whatever that one model can do, and on longer or more demanding exchanges its ceiling shows.

Janitor AI's defining feature is **bring-your-own-model (BYOM)**. It has a free in-house option (Janitor LLM) that's fine for casual browsing, but the real unlock is connecting your own Claude or OpenAI API key. The same character card that feels flat on the in-house model can feel genuinely alive when a frontier model like Claude is doing the writing — instructions hold longer, prose develops more texture, and the conversation sustains complexity that a lighter model simplifies away. That's covered in depth in our [full Janitor AI review](/blog/janitor-ai).

The trade-off is friction: BYOM takes about ten minutes to set up and, if you don't already have an API key, it's a decision to make. Character.AI asks nothing of you — you sign up and start talking. That gap in ease is real and runs the other way from the quality gap.

## The Character Library

Both platforms live or die on user-created characters, and both have huge catalogues.

Character.AI's library is large and more moderated, which means a more consistent floor — fewer broken or low-effort cards surface prominently — but also fewer of the edgier or more experimental personas, because the moderation removes them.

Janitor AI's catalogue is enormous and far less curated. There is almost certainly a card for whatever niche you're after, across every genre and content rating, but popularity doesn't reliably mean quality, and finding a genuinely good card takes a session or two of developing an eye for it. The skill that matters is reading a card before you commit: the specificity of the personality field and the quality of the example dialogue predict session quality better than the download count does. More on that in the [review](/blog/janitor-ai).

## Memory: A Tie, and Not in a Good Way

Here neither platform wins, because both share the same underlying limit.

Both are character-chat tools bounded by the model's context window, which means both eventually forget earlier details, drift out of personality, and lose the thread on a long session. Character.AI's memory limits are a frequent, long-standing complaint. Janitor AI is in the same boat — though connecting a frontier model with a large context window through BYOM pushes the wall further out than Character.AI can.

If memory is your actual priority — characters and world facts that stay consistent across a whole campaign — the honest answer is that *neither* of these is the right tool, and a database-backed platform like [RoleForge](/clients/roleforge) will beat both. The reason every chat-based AI hits this wall is worth understanding: see [why AI campaigns fall apart](/blog/why-llms-forget).

## Price

Both are free to start. Character.AI is free with an optional paid tier (faster responses, priority access). Janitor AI is free on its in-house model; the cost question only appears with BYOM, where you pay the per-token API cost of the model you connect — typically a few cents for a normal conversation if you already hold an API key, which is usually cheaper than a second subscription. If you already pay for Claude Pro or ChatGPT Plus, the BYOM math is favourable.

## Which Should You Choose?

A quick filter:

**Choose Character.AI if** you want the easiest possible start, you're on mobile, you value a polished app, and a safe-by-default filter is fine — or a positive — for how you play. It's the better casual, beginner, and on-the-go option.

**Choose Janitor AI if** the filter is what frustrated you, you want frontier-model writing quality, and you're willing to spend ten minutes on BYOM setup to get it. It's the better pick for serious roleplayers who want freedom and control. **[Full Janitor AI review](/blog/janitor-ai)** (rated 4/5).

**Choose neither if** what you actually want is a *game* — persistent memory, mechanics, stakes that carry across sessions — rather than a conversation. Both of these are chat platforms by design. The [Character.AI alternatives guide](/blog/character-ai-alternatives) covers the options that go further: database-backed RPGs, local setups via [SillyTavern](/blog/sillytavern-guide), and engineered games you run on a model you already pay for.

What neither offers is an engineered, LLM-native game that runs inside the frontier model you already use — no new account, no platform between you and the model. ## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Janitor AI better than Character.AI?**
For most roleplayers in 2026, yes — Janitor AI has a lighter content filter and lets you bring your own model (Claude or GPT) for far better writing. Character.AI is more polished, more beginner-friendly, and has a stronger mobile app, but its strict filter and limited memory are the two things that send most people looking for an alternative.

**Is Janitor AI more uncensored than Character.AI?**
Yes. Character.AI runs a strict, centralised content filter that interrupts not just explicit content but conflict, romance, and dark themes. Janitor AI's content policy is meaningfully lighter, and connecting your own model widens what it will engage with further. For the full range of no-filter options, see the [uncensored AI roleplay guide](/blog/uncensored-ai-roleplay).

**Is Janitor AI free like Character.AI?**
Both have free tiers. Character.AI is free with an optional paid tier for faster responses. Janitor AI is free on its in-house model; the bring-your-own-model feature adds only the per-token API cost of whatever model you connect, which is usually a few cents per session if you already have an API key.

**Can Janitor AI use the same characters as Character.AI?**
Not by direct import, but Janitor AI's character-card format is the open standard most of this space uses, and its library is enormous. You can also recreate a Character.AI persona by writing its personality, voice, and example dialogue into a new card — and on Janitor AI's lighter filter, that card often performs better than the original.

**Does Janitor AI or Character.AI have better memory?**
Neither is built for long-term memory — both are bounded by the model's context window and forget earlier details over a long session. Janitor AI's memory improves when you connect a frontier model with a large context window. For genuine persistence across a campaign, a database-backed platform like [RoleForge](/clients/roleforge) beats both.

**Should I use Janitor AI, Character.AI, or something else?**
Use Character.AI for casual, mobile-first, safe-by-default character chat. Use Janitor AI if you want a lighter filter and frontier-model writing quality and don't mind a steeper learning curve.
