**Janitor AI vs Friends & Fables** puts two of the most-searched names in AI roleplay side by side — but they were built to answer different questions. Janitor AI answers "can I talk to this character?" with the largest community card library in the space. Friends & Fables answers "can an AI actually run a game?" with an orchestrated Game Master, hex-map combat, and a memory system built specifically not to forget. One is a conversation. The other is a campaign.

This breaks down what actually separates them — character depth, memory, multiplayer, model flexibility, and price — so you can tell which problem you actually have.

The one-line summary: **Janitor AI is the better library; Friends & Fables is the better game.** The detail behind that is below.

## What Each One Actually Is

[Janitor AI](/clients/janitor) is a character library with a model layer underneath it — not a game engine, not a VTT, not a narrative framework. Users browse or create character cards and launch a chat session against one. The distinguishing feature is bring-your-own-model: connect an API key from Claude, OpenAI, or another provider and run the entire catalogue on whichever model you already trust. Our [full Janitor AI review](/blog/janitor-ai) rates it 4/5 — the best character library that exists, scoped correctly.

[Friends & Fables](/clients/friends-and-fables) is the current high-water mark for feature density in AI tabletop gaming. Its Game Master, Franz, isn't a single chatbot — it's an orchestration layer that routes each turn to up to a dozen specialized models, one for lore, one for the character sheet, one for map coordinates, one for narrative, before assembling the result. That's why it can run a genuine ruleset instead of just describing one. Our [full Friends & Fables review](/blog/friends-and-fables-review) rates it 4.5/5, the highest-rated platform in our directory.

## Character Depth vs Campaign Depth

Janitor AI's strength is breadth and specificity of *character*. The catalogue spans hundreds of thousands of community-built cards across every genre and archetype — if you want a specific persona to talk to, the odds someone has already built it are high, and no curated platform matches that variety.

Friends & Fables' strength is breadth and specificity of *campaign*. A full V3 combat system with dynamic hex battlemaps, line-of-sight and cover calculations, damage resistances and vulnerabilities, plus sprawling player-built worlds ranging from X-Men simulations to grimdark MorkBorg adaptations. It has no character-card library in the Janitor AI sense — it has a world for a party to inhabit instead.

If what draws you to a platform is inhabiting a relationship with one specific character, Janitor AI has no real equivalent in Friends & Fables. If what draws you is a tactical, consequence-driven world with a party, Friends & Fables has no real equivalent in Janitor AI.

## Memory and Consistency

This is the widest gap between the two, and it isn't close. Janitor AI keeps **no memory beyond the current context window** — it has no state persistence, no mechanical layer, and treats each message as a generative response to whatever context still fits. Log off and come back, and continuity depends entirely on what got carried forward (see [why LLMs forget](/blog/why-llms-forget) for why this happens on every chat-style platform).

Friends & Fables was built specifically to close that gap. Its Working Context system filters and carries forward only the lore, character states, and environmental facts relevant to the current scene before Franz generates a response. The NPC you built a relationship with three sessions ago still remembers it; the sword you lost to a river doesn't quietly reappear. For campaigns measured in dozens of sessions rather than one long chat, this is the difference that compounds.

## Multiplayer and Social Play

Janitor AI supports a Groups feature for multi-character scenes — several AI personas in one conversation thread — but it's fundamentally a solo-facing tool, and long group threads can see distinct character voices blur under a single context window, especially on lighter models.

Friends & Fables supports real synchronous multiplayer for up to six players, with individual character tracking, split-party management, and shared tactical maps. If you want to play *with other people* rather than alongside AI personas, this isn't a close contest.

## Model Flexibility and Filters

Janitor AI's bring-your-own-model design is architecturally significant: the character layer is Janitor's, the model is yours. Players already paying for Claude or ChatGPT can run the entire card library through it at just the API token cost, and the character-card layer mediates requests in ways that meaningfully loosen restrictions compared to using a model's native interface directly.

Friends & Fables offers no equivalent model choice — Franz's multi-LLM orchestration is the platform's own fixed backend, not something a player swaps out. Its content controls run through platform-level tone and rating settings rather than a bring-your-own-filter approach. If model choice and lighter restrictions are what you're after specifically, that's a Janitor AI strength Friends & Fables doesn't compete on.

## Ease of Starting and Price

Janitor AI wins the on-ramp and the free end decisively. It's free on the in-house Janitor LLM, or pay-per-use if you connect your own API key — often just a few cents a session on a model like Claude Sonnet. You're chatting within minutes of creating an account.

Friends & Fables is a premium product with only a limited trial. Meaningful play means subscribing: roughly $19.95/month (Starter, four players) up to $39.95/month (Legend, six players), with two months free on annual billing. It also asks more of the player — it's not a system that runs cleanly on autopilot, and it can occasionally force encounters where a social scene would land better. For a group replacing a standing tabletop night, the math works. For a casual solo dabble, it's a lot of commitment for a conversation you could have on Janitor AI for free.
