"Best free AI roleplay" is one of the most searched phrases in this space, and also one of the most misleading — because **free** means at least four different things, and the gap between them is the difference between a great free experience and a demo designed to make you subscribe. Before the rankings, it's worth thirty seconds on what you're actually being offered, because it changes which option is right for you.

## What "Free" Actually Means

There are four kinds of free in AI roleplay, and they're not equal:

1. **Genuinely free, no catch.** The whole thing is free to use, with no subscription gating the real experience. Rare, and the most valuable.
2. **Free tier with limits.** The platform is free to start but caps generations, memory, or features, with the good stuff behind a paywall. Fine for trying; frustrating for living in.
3. **Free software, your hardware.** Open-source and free to run, but you supply the model and the machine. Free in money, costly in setup.
4. **Free on a model you already have.** You bring ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini — even their free tiers — and add a game on top at no extra cost.

Most "best free" lists blur these together. This one sorts by them, because "free RPG that runs on the AI I already pay nothing for" and "free trial that nags me at turn ten" are completely different products.

## 1. Arcanum Originals — Best Genuinely-Free RPGs (No Subscription)

If you want the deepest free experience with the cleanest definition of free, the **[Arcanum Originals](/arcanum-games)** are it. They're free downloads — an engineered game engine in a couple of files — that you load into ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini and play. There's no platform, no account, and no subscription: the only thing you need is a model, and the free tiers of the major models work (larger knowledge files benefit from paid tiers, but the games run without them).

That makes them "free on a model you already have" *and* genuinely free at the same time — the rare overlap. And they're not thin: [Aevum Realm Architect](/arcanum-games/aevum) is a deterministic kingdom-builder, [Star Freighter Drift](/arcanum-games/star-freighter-drift) a space-trading RPG with a real economy, [The Chronicler](/arcanum-games/the-chronicler) a relationship-driven engine, and [Eirathis Strider](/arcanum-games/eirathis) a lore-rich adventure. Nothing else on this list gives you this much engineered depth for exactly zero.

**Kind of free:** genuinely free + runs on free model tiers
**Best for:** deep, structured solo campaigns with no subscription

## 2. RoleForge — Best Free Hosted Platform

If you'd rather a platform handle everything, **[RoleForge](/clients/roleforge)** is the standout free option — currently in closed alpha, and unrated until public release per our pre-release policy. It's free, and its headline feature is the thing most free platforms lack: real persistent memory. A SQL database holds the actual state of your world and characters, and the LLM narrates what the database says happened — so a long campaign stays consistent instead of quietly forgetting itself.

That a platform this capable-on-paper is free is genuinely unusual. **[Preview](/blog/roleforge-preview)** (currently in closed alpha — unrated until public release, not yet tested by us).

**Kind of free:** genuinely free (hosted)
**Best for:** long solo campaigns that actually remember you

## 3. SillyTavern — Best Free for Total Control

**[SillyTavern](/blog/sillytavern-guide)** is free and open-source, and it gives you more control than any paid platform: your model, your memory system, your filter (or none), your data. Run an open-weight model locally and the whole thing costs nothing beyond electricity, with no content restrictions and full privacy.

The catch is the third kind of free — you supply the hardware (a capable GPU) or wire up an API, and the first hour is setup. Free in money, not in effort. If you have the machine and the patience, nothing matches it. **[Full guide](/blog/sillytavern-guide)**.

**Kind of free:** free software, your hardware
**Best for:** power users who want control and privacy at no cost

## 4. AI Dungeon — Best Free Freeform Sandbox

**[AI Dungeon](/blog/is-ai-dungeon-free)**'s free tier became genuinely usable after the Unchained update: ad-free play on its in-house models without the old energy systems. If you want to improvise an open-ended story with zero setup and zero cost, it's the easiest door in — type anything and go. The limits are writing quality and memory on the free model, and the premium tiers get expensive, but as a *free* sandbox it delivers.

**Kind of free:** free tier (usable)
**Best for:** instant, unrestricted freeform storytelling

## 5. Taverna — Best Free AI DM for Discord

**[Taverna](/clients/taverna)** runs a full 5e-style AI Game Master inside a Discord server you already have — free, no new app, no new accounts. It handles initiative, combat, inventory, and leveling through slash commands. The trade-off is that a text channel isn't a battlemap, so spatial combat is abstracted, but for a group that lives in Discord and lost its DM, it's a free, frictionless answer. **[Full review](/blog/taverna-review)** (rated 4/5).

**Kind of free:** genuinely free
**Best for:** Discord groups who want an AI DM in their existing server

## 6. Infinity DM — Best Free Async Group Play

**[Infinity DM](/clients/infinity-dm)** is a free, asynchronous AI RPG built for adults who can't commit to a synchronous session — take your turn today, let your party take theirs tomorrow. For busy groups, the async-by-design structure is the whole appeal, and it costs nothing to play. **[Full review](/blog/infinity-dm-review)** (rated 3.5/5).

**Kind of free:** genuinely free
**Best for:** busy groups playing on their own schedule

## Free Character Chat: Janitor AI & Character.AI

If it's character *chat* you want for free, both [Janitor AI](/clients/janitor) and [Character.AI](/clients/characterai) have free tiers with enormous libraries. Be clear on the catch, though: both run weaker in-house models for free (Janitor AI nudges you toward bringing your own model, which adds token cost), and Character.AI's free experience is heavily filtered. They're free to start; the *good* version of each usually isn't. For where to take character chat next, see [Janitor AI alternatives](/blog/janitor-ai-alternatives).

## Roll Your Own — Free on the AI You Already Use

The most overlooked free option is the one you already have. If you use ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini — even on a free tier — you can run a complete roleplay yourself; the only missing piece is a good system prompt. The free [AI RPG Prompt Builder](/tools/prompt-builder) assembles one for you: pick genre, tone, and memory approach and paste the result straight in. Pair it with the free [Campaign Memory Tool](/tools/campaign-memory) to manage long-campaign memory by hand, and you have a complete, genuinely free setup with no new platform at all. For a finished engine instead of a prompt, that's what the [Arcanum Originals](/arcanum-games) are.
