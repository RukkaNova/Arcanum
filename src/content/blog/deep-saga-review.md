[Deep Saga](/games/deep-saga) is a Custom GPT by John Rowlands that lets you pick a genre — Fantasy, Sci-Fi, or Horror — then a specific Saga and a character, and drops you into a scene-by-scene adventure with real stakes and a generated illustration attached to every beat. It's popular enough to have spun off standalone iOS and Android apps alongside the GPT itself, and after a few playthroughs I understand both the appeal and the ceiling in the same breath: this is a well-instructed prompt doing exactly what a prompt can do, no more.

## What Deep Saga Does

Character creation is quick and structured: choose your genre, choose a Saga within it, choose a character, and you're in. From there Deep Saga runs the whole thing as game master — narrating the scene, generating an image to match it, and presenting three choices at each turn. The prompt is precise about its own presentation, too; small formatting quirks like always capitalizing "Saga" show a level of instruction-following that a looser prompt wouldn't bother enforcing.

It also doesn't pull punches. Failure and character death are genuinely on the table, which matters more than it sounds — a lot of Custom GPT RPGs quietly protect the player from real consequences, and Deep Saga doesn't. Losing a Saga means losing it.

## The Pictures Aren't the Selling Point They Once Were

The headline feature is the per-scene image generation, and it's a pleasant touch — most text-first Custom GPTs don't bother. But it's worth being honest about what that feature is actually worth in 2026: image generation is trivial to attach to any GPT now, so this isn't the differentiator it might have been when Deep Saga first launched. Judge it on the writing and the structure underneath the pictures, because that's what you're actually playing.

## The Prompt-Only Ceiling

Here's the honest limitation. Deep Saga ships as a single master prompt — no reference files, no knowledge base, nothing to fall back on beyond its own instructions. That puts it in company with every other prompt-only Custom GPT: instant to start, and the writing holds up fine for a sitting, but there's no canon behind the curtain to anchor it once a session runs long. [Solo RPG Master](/blog/solo-rpg-master-review) hits the same wall for the same structural reason, and [why LLMs forget](/blog/why-llms-forget) covers why a prompt alone can't fix it. [8-Bit Kingdoms](/blog/8-bit-kingdoms-review), reviewed the same week as this one, takes the opposite bet — worth reading side by side to see what loading real reference files actually buys a game.

## Who It's For

Play Deep Saga if you want a quick, dangerous, illustrated adventure across a genre of your choosing, with no setup beyond picking a Saga. It's a good default when you want stakes and a visual layer without loading knowledge files into anything.

Look elsewhere if you're after a game with real mechanical depth or a world that was designed rather than requested — the wider [best ChatGPT RPG games](/blog/best-chatgpt-rpg-games) round-up and the [public games directory](/games) both cover games that go further on that front.

## Verdict

Deep Saga is a competent, well-instructed prompt-only RPG that delivers exactly what it promises — genre choice, real stakes, and a picture with every scene — without pretending to be more than that. The images are a nice-to-have rather than a game-changer, and the lack of reference files caps how deep any single playthrough can go.

**Deep Saga Rating: 3/5** — A solid prompt-only ChatGPT RPG with real stakes and per-scene art, held to the same ceiling as every other generalist Custom GPT with no knowledge files behind it.
