If you're choosing between **AI Dungeon and Character.AI**, the most useful thing to know up front is that they're not really competing for the same job. [AI Dungeon](/clients/ai-dungeon) is a freeform story sandbox — you type anything and an AI improvises a world around you. [Character.AI](/clients/characterai) is a character-chat platform — you pick a persona from an enormous library and have a conversation with it. One is about *playing a story*; the other is about *talking to a character*.

That's why "which is better" has no single answer until you know which of those two things you came for. This is a head-to-head on the dimensions that actually decide it — creative freedom, the filter, writing quality, memory, the library, and price — and an honest look at the case where the right answer is neither of them.

## Story vs Character: The Distinction That Decides It

Start here, because it determines whether the rest even matters.

AI Dungeon hands you a world and a blank slate. There's no fixed protagonist to talk to — *you* are the author and the player at once, typing actions and dialogue while the AI plays everything else: the setting, the cast, the consequences. It's collaborative fiction with you in the driver's seat. If the fantasy is "I want to go on an adventure and see where it leads," AI Dungeon is built for exactly that.

Character.AI hands you a person. The entire experience is organised around one-on-one conversation with a specific persona — a companion, a mentor, a fictional figure, a celebrity. You're not driving a plot so much as inhabiting a relationship. If the fantasy is "I want to talk to *this* character," Character.AI is built for that.

Most disappointment with either platform comes from arriving wanting the other thing. Players who want a story find Character.AI's chat format aimless; players who want a specific character find AI Dungeon's freeform sandbox unfocused. Get this distinction right and you've mostly made your decision.

## Creative Freedom and the Filter

This is the sharpest practical difference between them.

AI Dungeon's defining feature has always been freedom. You can attempt almost anything — genres collide, plots go sideways, the absurd and the dark are both on the table — and the platform largely lets you. For players who want their fiction to go wherever their imagination does, that openness is the whole appeal.

Character.AI sits at the opposite end. Its content moderation is strict and quick to fire, and not only on explicit material — conflict, romance, violence, and morally complex scenes can all get cut off the instant they get interesting, replaced by a deflection that breaks the spell. It's a defensible choice for a mainstream, younger audience, but for storytellers who want real stakes it's the single most common reason people leave. If the filter is your dealbreaker, AI Dungeon is by far the freer of the two — and for the full range of no-filter options that go further still, see the [uncensored AI roleplay guide](/blog/uncensored-ai-roleplay).

One honest caveat on AI Dungeon: its history isn't spotless. A past privacy controversy around manual review of stories still colours its reputation for some long-time users. It's a different concern from Character.AI's live filtering, but worth knowing going in.

## Writing Quality

Neither platform is a frontier-model showcase, and both show it differently.

AI Dungeon's output is serviceable but frequently criticised as repetitive — cliché dialogue, generic fantasy description, and narrative padding, especially on its lower-tier models. Its best writing is locked behind expensive premium tiers. Character.AI runs only its own proprietary model, tuned for character chat: consistent and rarely nonsensical in conversation, but capped by what that single model can do and with no option to bring a stronger one. Call it a wash — AI Dungeon can climb higher if you pay a lot; Character.AI is steadier but lower-ceilinged. Anyone used to a frontier model will notice the difference on both.

## Memory: A Tie, and Not the Good Kind

Here neither wins, because both fight the same losing battle.

Both are bounded by the model's context window, so both eventually forget earlier details, drift out of character, and lose the thread on a long session. AI Dungeon's continuity is inconsistent on extended campaigns, with stronger memory reserved for its highest-priced tiers. Character.AI's memory is simply weak, which is especially damaging for a tool whose entire value is sustaining a believable persona. The reason every chat-based AI hits this wall is worth understanding — see [why AI campaigns fall apart](/blog/why-llms-forget) — but the practical upshot is that if long-term consistency is your priority, *neither* of these is the right tool, and a database-backed platform like [RoleForge](/clients/roleforge) will beat both.

## The Library

This is the axis where the two are least alike.

Character.AI's library is its crown jewel: the biggest catalogue of user-made characters anywhere, a first-mover advantage compounded over years. Whatever persona you have in mind, it's almost certainly there, often in several versions. For breadth of *characters*, nothing matches it.

AI Dungeon isn't a character library at all — it's a scenario engine. There are community-made worlds and starting scenarios, but the real "library" is your own imagination plus a prompt that accepts almost anything. So the comparison is apples to oranges: Character.AI wins decisively if you want to browse and pick a character; AI Dungeon wins if you'd rather conjure a situation from nothing.

## Price

Both are free to start, which matters. Character.AI is free with an optional paid tier (c.ai+) for faster responses during peak times — and notably, paying doesn't lift the two things that actually limit it, the filter and the memory. AI Dungeon is free and genuinely playable on its Griffin model after the Unchained update, but its best models and memory live in premium tiers that climb to roughly $50–100/month at the top — the priciest option in this matchup, and a frequent target of criticism for the value it returns. For free, casual use, both are reasonable; at the high end, AI Dungeon asks the most.

## Which Should You Choose?

A quick filter:

**Choose AI Dungeon if** you want to play and improvise a freeform story, value total creative freedom over structure, and want a world you drive rather than a character you message. It's the better storytelling sandbox, and its free tier is the easy entry point. **[Full AI Dungeon review](/blog/ai-dungeon-review)** (3.5/5).

**Choose Character.AI if** you want the smoothest possible way to chat with a specific character, you're on mobile, and a safe-by-default filter is fine for how you play. Just know that [Janitor AI](/blog/janitor-ai) does Character.AI's exact job with a lighter filter and bring-your-own-model support — we break that down in [Janitor AI vs Character.AI](/blog/janitor-ai-vs-character-ai). **[Full Character.AI review](/blog/characterai-review)** (2/5).

**Choose neither if** what you actually want is a *game* — persistent memory, mechanics, and stakes that carry across sessions — rather than an improvised story or a conversation. AI Dungeon is a sandbox and Character.AI is a chat; neither is a structured game by design. The [AI Dungeon alternatives](/blog/ai-dungeon-alternatives) and [Character.AI alternatives](/blog/character-ai-alternatives) guides map the options that go further.

## The Option Neither One Offers

Both AI Dungeon and Character.AI are platforms you log into and play inside. What neither offers is an engineered, LLM-native *game* that runs on the frontier model you already use — no platform sitting between you and the model, no ceiling set by an in-house engine. That's what the [Arcanum Originals](/arcanum-games) are: you bring ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini, and the game is a couple of files you load in. The flagship, [Aevum Realm Architect](/blog/aevum-realm-architect), runs a deterministic kingdom-builder with a hidden state tracker and drift-correction built in — the persistent structure that a freeform sandbox and a character chat both lack. It's a different category from either platform here, but if "I want it to be a *real* game" is the itch, it's the one that scratches it. ## Frequently Asked Questions

They're built for different things, so neither is simply better. AI Dungeon is the better tool if you want to play and improvise a freeform story in a world you drive. Character.AI is the better tool if you want to chat with a specific character and value a polished, beginner-friendly app. For serious roleplay, though, AI Dungeon's creative freedom gives it the edge, while Character.AI is held back by a strict filter and a better-executed competitor in [Janitor AI](/blog/janitor-ai).

**What is the difference between AI Dungeon and Character.AI?**
AI Dungeon is a freeform text-adventure sandbox: you type anything and the AI continues the story, with no fixed character and almost no restrictions. Character.AI is a character-chat platform: you pick a persona from a huge library and have a one-on-one conversation with it. One is about playing a story; the other is about talking to a character.

**Is AI Dungeon or Character.AI better for roleplay?**
It depends on the kind of roleplay. For open-ended, do-anything storytelling and worlds with no filter getting in the way, AI Dungeon is better. For inhabiting a relationship with one specific character, Character.AI is purpose-built for it — though Janitor AI does that same job with a lighter filter and stronger models. Neither runs a structured game with persistent stakes.

**Which is more uncensored, AI Dungeon or Character.AI?**
AI Dungeon, by a wide margin. Its whole identity is creative freedom — you can take a story almost anywhere. Character.AI runs a strict content filter that interrupts not just explicit material but conflict, romance, and dark themes. If filtering is your dealbreaker, AI Dungeon is the freer of the two, though dedicated no-filter options go further still.

**Is Character.AI or AI Dungeon free?**
Both have genuinely usable free tiers. Character.AI is free with an optional paid tier for faster responses during busy periods. AI Dungeon is free on its Griffin model after the Unchained update, but its best models and memory are locked behind premium tiers that run as high as roughly $50 to $100 per month — the most expensive option in this comparison.

**Should I use AI Dungeon, Character.AI, or something else?**
Use AI Dungeon to improvise a freeform story with total creative freedom. Use Character.AI for the easiest way to chat with a specific character, or Janitor AI for that same job done better. If what you actually want is a structured game with mechanics, persistent memory, and stakes that carry across sessions, neither fits — an engineered system you run on a frontier model serves you far better.
