**Deep Realms vs Friends & Fables** puts our two highest-rated AI RPG platforms side by side, and they could hardly be built around more different premises. Friends & Fables is the most visually and mechanically ambitious AI tabletop on the market — hex battlemaps, a dozen specialized models orchestrated per turn, a genuine VTT experience. Deep Realms is the opposite bet: no maps, no portraits, a Python console instead of a character sheet, and a ceiling that's limited only by what you're willing to code.

Both rate near the top of our directory. Neither is trying to be the other. This breaks down where they actually diverge — content freedom, customization, memory, accessibility, and multiplayer — so you can tell which one matches how you actually want to play.

The one-line summary: **Deep Realms is the deeper, unrestricted power tool; Friends & Fables is the more complete, polished game.** The detail behind that is below.

## What Each One Actually Is

[Deep Realms](/clients/deep-realms) is built around a Python-based "World Engine" that weaves quantitative mechanics — hit points, mana thresholds, numerical skill checks — directly into generative text, without a traditional graphical character sheet. Its proprietary models (Freya 4.1, Magni, Aion, Astral) are tuned for long-form narrative coherence rather than speed. Our [full Deep Realms review](/blog/deep-realms-review) rates it 4/5 — the highest ceiling in the space for the specific player it's built for.

[Friends & Fables](/clients/friends-and-fables) is the current high-water mark for feature density in AI tabletop gaming. Its Game Master, Franz, routes each turn through up to a dozen specialized models before assembling a response, powering a genuine V3 hex-battlemap combat system rather than a description of one. Our [full Friends & Fables review](/blog/friends-and-fables-review) rates it 4.5/5, the highest-rated platform in our directory.

## Content Freedom and Filters

This is one of the widest gaps between them. Deep Realms explicitly permits mature and NSFW content when players select its less-restricted models — Astral and Magni specifically — a real, stated design choice rather than a loophole. It's one of the platforms players move to specifically after another tool's filters interrupted a legitimate dramatic scene (see our [uncensored AI roleplay guide](/blog/uncensored-ai-roleplay) for the full landscape).

Friends & Fables offers no equivalent model-level choice. Its content controls run through platform-level tone and rating settings rather than a bring-your-own-filter approach, and Franz's multi-LLM orchestration is a fixed backend the player doesn't swap out. If unrestricted content specifically is what you're after, that's a Deep Realms strength Friends & Fables doesn't compete on.

## Customization and Depth

Deep Realms' Python World Engine is the platform's entire identity. If you can write a function that checks a stat threshold and passes a result back to the narrative layer, you can build custom mechanics, persistent environmental variables, and combat systems designed around your specific world's logic — a ceiling nothing else in this comparison touches. The tradeoff is the entry price: reaching that ceiling requires actual code, and the base experience without it is solid but comparatively unremarkable for what you're paying.

Friends & Fables' depth runs in a different direction — a complete V3 combat system with dynamic hex battlemaps, line-of-sight and cover calculations, damage resistances and vulnerabilities, plus sprawling player-built worlds from X-Men simulations to grimdark MorkBorg adaptations. None of it requires code. All of it is built in, at the cost of not being able to bend the system to a use case its designers didn't anticipate.

## Memory and Consistency

Both platforms treat memory as a first-class problem, and both solve it well — differently. Deep Realms' proprietary models process up to roughly 80,000 words of active context, vastly outperforming standard context windows; for sprawling, lore-dense campaigns, that headroom is the difference between a coherent world and a collapsing one.

Friends & Fables' Working Context system takes a more surgical approach: rather than maximizing raw capacity, it filters and carries forward only the lore, character states, and environmental facts relevant to the current scene before Franz generates a response, and the result is transparent and player-editable. Deep Realms wins on sheer capacity; Friends & Fables wins on precision and control over what the AI is actually working with.

## Visual Layer and Accessibility

Here Friends & Fables wins outright. Its hex battlemaps, voice commands, and tactical visualization are the closest any AI platform comes to replicating a physical table in a browser. Deep Realms has no equivalent — no grid maps, no consistent character portraits, no dynamic scene visualization. It's a text-and-image-generation platform, and the image generation is a bolt-on feature rather than an integrated system.

That gap cuts both ways. Deep Realms' onboarding is functional rather than welcoming, and the Python requirement will eliminate a large share of players before they ever get to the World Engine's actual ceiling. Friends & Fables asks for a different kind of investment — no code, but real engagement with a system that rewards attention and can occasionally force encounters it shouldn't.

## Multiplayer

Friends & Fables is built for group play: real synchronous multiplayer up to six players, with individual character tracking, split-party management, and shared tactical maps. Deep Realms is fundamentally a solo-first tool; its Studio tier ($29/month) adds team collaboration, premium exports, and version control, but that's asynchronous worldbuilding collaboration, not a live shared session. If your table wants to play together in real time, Friends & Fables isn't close on this axis.

## Price

Deep Realms runs Free (limited world slots) through Pro at $9/month (unlimited worlds, full map builder, advanced AI) to Studio at $29/month (team collaboration). Friends & Fables runs Free (3 players, 5–25 turns/day) through Starter at $19.95/month (4 players) to Legend at $39.95/month (6 players), with two months free annually. For a solo player, Deep Realms is dramatically cheaper for comparable depth. For a group, Friends & Fables' per-seat pricing is the cost of the tactical layer it delivers.
