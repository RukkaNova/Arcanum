**Taverna vs Friends & Fables** is a comparison between two AI dungeon masters that agree on almost nothing except the goal: run a real D&D-style campaign without a human DM. Taverna's entire premise is meeting players where they already are — it lives inside Discord, the platform your group probably already uses to talk. Friends & Fables' premise is the opposite: build the most complete AI tabletop possible, and ask players to come to it.

Neither approach is wrong. This breaks down where they actually diverge — onboarding friction, tactical depth, access, privacy, and price — so you can tell which one your table should actually try.

## The Short Version

| | Taverna | Friends & Fables |
|---|---|---|
| **What it is** | D&D 5e AI DM running as a Discord bot | Structured AI tabletop / VTT |
| **Where it lives** | Inside your existing Discord server | Its own dedicated web platform |
| **Onboarding** | Invite the bot, run a slash command | Create an account, learn the interface |
| **Tactical combat** | Text-narrated, no grid | Full hex battlemap, line-of-sight, cover |
| **Access** | Closed beta, invite-only | Open to anyone today |
| **Privacy** | OpenAI confirmed no training on API data | Platform-level, not independently confirmed |
| **Price** | Free during beta; post-launch TBD | Free tier / $19.95–$39.95/month |
| **Arcanum rating** | [Closed beta (unrated)](/blog/taverna-review) | [4.5/5](/blog/friends-and-fables-review) |

The one-line summary: **Taverna is the frictionless Discord-native DM; Friends & Fables is the deepest tactical AI tabletop.** The detail behind that is below.

## What Each One Actually Is

[Taverna](/clients/taverna) is deployed entirely as a Discord bot by Scalar Studio. It uses OpenAI's API to run a full fantasy campaign — combat turn orders, group inventory, character leveling — directly in your server's text channels, adjudicated via natural language and slash commands. No website to visit, no account to create. Our [full Taverna review](/blog/taverna-review) covers it in depth — it's still closed beta, so per our pre-release policy there's no rating yet.

[Friends & Fables](/clients/friends-and-fables) is the current high-water mark for feature density in AI tabletop gaming. Its Game Master, Franz, routes each player turn through up to a dozen specialized models before assembling a response, powering a genuine V3 hex-battlemap combat system. Our [full Friends & Fables review](/blog/friends-and-fables-review) rates it 4.5/5, the highest-rated platform in our directory.

New to the category? Our [AI D&D guide](/blog/ai-dnd) covers the wider landscape of playing D&D-style campaigns with AI, solo or in a group.

## Onboarding and Friction

This is Taverna's entire reason for existing. Most AI RPG platforms ask a group to leave the app they already use, create new accounts, and agree to learn an unfamiliar interface together — friction that kills more group AI RPG attempts than any feature gap does. Taverna eliminates that step: invite the bot, set up a channel, run a slash command, and a group decision made on a Friday can have a session running that same evening.

Friends & Fables asks for real investment by comparison — a new account, a genuine interface to learn, and a system that rewards engagement rather than running on autopilot. That investment buys real capability, but it is investment, and it's the tradeoff Taverna was explicitly built to avoid.

## Tactical Depth

Here the position flips completely. Friends & Fables runs a full V3 combat system: dynamic hex battlemaps, line-of-sight calculations, cover bonuses, damage resistances and vulnerabilities. For groups that want the precision of spatial tactical combat, nothing in this comparison touches it.

Taverna is constrained by its own format — a Discord text channel isn't a battlemap, and tactical positioning is abstracted through narration rather than shown on a grid. The comparison isn't entirely fair to Taverna, since it was never trying to replace a physical gaming table the way Friends & Fables is — it was trying to add a DM to a group that's already playing socially in Discord. But if your table cares about precise grid combat, Taverna genuinely can't provide it.

## Access and Availability

Friends & Fables is open to anyone today — create an account and start on the free tier immediately. Taverna, as of mid-2026, is in closed beta and requires an application through its official website. This is the single biggest practical barrier to trying it: not the feature set, not the price, but simply getting in. Groups that want to evaluate an AI RPG on their own timeline without waiting for an invite will find Friends & Fables the more accessible starting point right now.

## Privacy

Taverna has one clear point in its favor here: because it runs on OpenAI's API, OpenAI has confirmed it does not train its models on data processed through the API. For groups running mature narratives or who want session content to stay genuinely private, that's a concrete, verifiable commitment. Friends & Fables' data handling is platform-level and not independently confirmed to the same degree — not necessarily worse, just less specifically documented.

## Price

Taverna is currently free during its invite-only beta, with post-launch pricing not yet confirmed — likely server-level or per-user tiers to offset the OpenAI API costs the bot runs on. Friends & Fables runs a straightforward subscription ladder: Free (3 players, 5–25 turns/day) through Starter at $19.95/month (4 players) to Legend at $39.95/month (6 players), with two months free annually. Until Taverna's launch pricing lands, it's the cheaper option by default — but that could change once it's out of beta.
