**Perchance and Janitor AI** end up compared constantly because they occupy overlapping territory in AI roleplay — free, permissive, no-frills-necessary character chat — while actually being built for slightly different jobs. Perchance is a fast, generator-driven creative playground; Janitor AI is a character-roleplay platform with real depth and model flexibility. After time with both, the two keep reminding you of each other, and it's worth being precise about where they actually diverge.

*(Perchance and Janitor AI are independent third-party platforms; Arcanum is unaffiliated with either, and names are used nominatively for identification.)*

## The Short Version

| | Perchance | Janitor AI |
|---|---|---|
| **Core strength** | Creative generation — text generators, randomizers, story prompts | Character roleplay — immersive, ongoing conversations |
| **Setup** | Zero — no account, no API key | Minimal for the in-house model; BYOM takes setup |
| **Character depth** | Basic — name, personality, scenario | Detailed personalities, backstories, behavioral traits |
| **Session length** | Best for short, casual sessions | Built for long-form, ongoing roleplay |
| **Model flexibility** | In-house AI Text Plugin only | In-house model **or** bring-your-own (Claude, GPT, etc.) |
| **Content restrictions** | Light — no blanket filter | Light — widens further with BYOM |
| **Price** | Free, no cap | Free on in-house model; BYOM adds token cost |
| **Rating** | **3/5** ([full review](/blog/perchance-review)) | **4/5** ([full review](/blog/janitor-ai)) |

The one-line summary: **Perchance is the better idea generator; Janitor AI is the better place to actually live inside a character.**

## What Perchance Is Better At

**Creative generation.** Perchance is built on top of a platform literally designed for random generators — text generators, randomizers, story prompts, and lightweight AI tools sit right alongside the chat itself. That heritage shows: it's a genuinely good tool for producing ideas, not just conversations.

**Fast experimentation.** You can create or remix a character and scenario in seconds, with no setup cost to abandoning one idea for the next. That makes Perchance a low-stakes sandbox in a way Janitor AI, with its heavier investment in card creation and BYOM configuration, isn't really trying to be.

**Scenario control.** Because there's no filter or account layer standing between you and the text engine, Perchance is useful specifically when you want to shape the prompt, the narration style, or the story framework directly — closer to steering a generator than negotiating with a chatbot.

**Accessibility.** Browser-based, no signup, works for a complete beginner immediately. Janitor AI's card-browsing and BYOM setup ask a little more of a first-time user, even though the core chat is also free.

## What Janitor AI Is Better At

**Character roleplay.** Janitor AI is built specifically around chatting with AI characters and sustaining an immersive conversation — that's the whole product, not a feature layered onto something else. It shows in the depth of what the platform supports.

**Character customization.** Detailed personalities, backstories, and behavioral traits are core to Janitor AI's character-card format, with a catalogue of hundreds of thousands of cards built around exactly that level of depth. Perchance's characters are comparatively lightweight — a name, personality, and scenario field, with far less structure for backstory and behavior.

**Long-form interaction.** Janitor AI is generally the better-suited platform for ongoing roleplay sessions and relationship-style character chats that continue across multiple sittings, helped by a much larger and more established character ecosystem.

**Model flexibility.** Advanced users can connect external language models or APIs through Janitor AI's bring-your-own-model support, meaningfully changing chat quality and behavior. Perchance runs entirely on its own in-house AI Text Plugin, with no equivalent option to swap in a different model.

## Memory: Both Limited, Janitor AI Ahead

Neither platform is built for genuine long-term memory — both are ultimately bounded by context-window limits, the same wall covered in [why AI campaigns fall apart](/blog/why-llms-forget). But the gap between them is real in practice. Perchance's memory, even with its Lore Box feature re-establishing key facts, is noticeably more inconsistent — sessions lose the thread faster and less predictably. Janitor AI's memory improves meaningfully when BYOM connects a frontier model with a large context window, pushing the wall further out than Perchance's fixed in-house engine can.

## Which Should You Choose?

**Choose Perchance if** you want a free, lightweight creative playground for generators, prompts, random story ideas, or a quick, casual AI chat with zero setup. **[Full Perchance review](/blog/perchance-review)** (rated 3/5).

**Choose Janitor AI if** your main goal is immersive character chat, long-form roleplay, and deeper control over character behavior — especially if you're willing to connect your own model. **[Full Janitor AI review](/blog/janitor-ai)** (rated 4/5).

**Use both.** This is a genuinely sensible combination, not a compromise: generate scenarios or character ideas quickly in Perchance, then take the concept to Janitor AI for the actual roleplay conversation, where the depth and customization can do more with it.

**Want a real game instead of a chat?** Neither platform tracks persistent world state or consequences across sessions. [RoleForge](/clients/roleforge) and [Friends & Fables](/clients/friends-and-fables) are built for that instead, and the free [Arcanum Originals](/arcanum-games) run structured campaigns on any frontier model you already pay for.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Perchance or Janitor AI better?**
They're built for different jobs. Perchance is the better creative sandbox — fast generators, scenario control, zero setup. Janitor AI is the better character-roleplay platform — deeper customization, longer-form sessions, and bring-your-own-model support. Neither is simply better; the right pick depends on whether you want to generate ideas or sustain a character conversation.

**Does Perchance have the same content restrictions as Janitor AI?**
Both are light on restrictions compared to mainstream chat apps. Perchance applies no general content filter beyond basic safety limits. Janitor AI's content policy is also permissive and widens further when you bring your own model. Practically, the two feel similar on this axis.

**Is Perchance free like Janitor AI?**
Both are free to use. Perchance has no paywall or message cap at all. Janitor AI is free on its in-house model, with bring-your-own-model adding only the per-token cost of whatever external model you connect.

**Which has better memory, Perchance or Janitor AI?**
Neither is strong, but Janitor AI has the edge, especially when connected to a frontier model with a large context window through BYOM. Perchance's memory — even with its Lore Box feature — is noticeably more inconsistent, losing track of established details faster.

**Can I use Perchance and Janitor AI together?**
Yes, and it's a genuinely sensible workflow: generate character ideas, scenarios, or plot seeds quickly in Perchance, then take the concept to Janitor AI for a longer, more customizable roleplay session.

**Should I use Perchance or Janitor AI for a long RPG campaign?**
Neither is really built for a long, structured campaign — both are conversational tools bounded by context-window memory. For genuine persistence across sessions, a database-backed platform like RoleForge or Friends & Fables is a better fit than either.
