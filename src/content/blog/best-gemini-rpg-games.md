## The Honest State of Gemini RPG in 2026

Search for "Gemini RPG" and you'll find developer experiments, API tutorials, and a handful of GitHub projects built to demonstrate Gemini's capabilities. What you won't find is a curated ecosystem of polished, playable experiences — because that ecosystem barely exists yet.

This isn't a criticism of the model. It's a reflection of where the LLM roleplay community put its energy. ChatGPT got Custom GPTs with a public store. Claude got Projects with a sophisticated knowledge-file system. Gemini got **Gems** — a genuinely capable feature that lets you package instructions and knowledge files into a persistent, reusable AI — but the **Google Gemini roleplay** community hasn't built for it at the same scale yet.

The gap is actually an opportunity. Players who do find and use **Gemini Gems for RPG** are operating in less crowded territory, and the model itself is more capable than its reputation in this space suggests. Here's what's available right now.

## 2. A Well-Crafted Custom Gemini Gem (DIY)

The second-best **Gemini RPG** experience available right now is one you build yourself — and **Gemini Gems** make this more achievable than most players realise.

A Gem packages a system prompt and knowledge files into a persistent, reusable AI that keeps its instructions active across every conversation. For roleplay, this means you define a GM persona, a world, a tone, and a ruleset once — and every session starts from that foundation without re-pasting anything. It is the cleanest system any frontier model offers for running a persistent campaign.

The practical steps: create a new Gem in Gemini Advanced, write a system prompt defining your world and the GM's behaviour, upload any world-building documents as knowledge files, and save. Gemini treats the knowledge files as reference material it actively consults rather than background context it has to hold in its working memory — one of the model's genuine strengths for sustained campaigns.

If you want a starting point rather than building from scratch, Eirathis Strider's master prompt is readable and instructive — it shows the architecture that makes a long-running **Google Gemini roleplay** campaign hold together. ## Why Gemini Specifically for Roleplay?

A fair question — given that Claude and ChatGPT have larger RPG communities, why run campaigns on **Google Gemini** at all? Three genuine reasons.

**The knowledge file handling.** Gemini treats uploaded files as reference material it actively consults rather than background context it has to hold in its context window. For games with large world bibles — like the Eirathis Atlas — this produces more consistent lore adherence than models that tend to drift away from their knowledge files over long sessions. The GM stays in the world you loaded, not the world it invents to fill gaps.

**The context window.** Gemini Advanced has one of the longest context windows of any frontier model, which directly translates to longer campaigns before the model starts losing the thread of earlier events. For players who want to run extended, multi-session campaigns — the kind where actions from session one still matter in session ten — this is a meaningful practical advantage over shorter-context alternatives.

**The instruction discipline.** For complex RPG prompts with many rules — agency protocols, HUD formats, resolution systems, proactivity engines — Gemini tends to follow structured instructions reliably across long sessions. The GM stays in character, the rules hold, and the format stays consistent. This is what separates a **Gemini Gems RPG** that holds together at turn 100 from one that's already unravelled at turn 30. Our [guide on why AI campaigns fall apart](/blog/why-llms-forget) covers why instruction discipline is the variable that matters most.

None of this makes Gemini objectively "the best" model for every player. Claude's prose quality and ChatGPT's ecosystem have their own genuine strengths. But for the specific demands of long, rules-heavy, lore-consistent campaigns, **Google Gemini roleplay** is a serious contender that deserves more attention than it gets.

## How to Set Up a Gemini Gem for RPG

If you haven't used Gems before, the setup takes under five minutes:

1. Open Gemini Advanced and find **Gems** in the sidebar. Open the Gem manager and select **New Gem**.
2. Give it a name matching your game.
3. Paste your master prompt into the **Instructions** field.
4. Use the **Add knowledge / upload files** option to attach any world files — the Atlas, the Star Chart, whatever the game uses.
5. Save the Gem, then select it to begin your first session.

The Gem persists across all your conversations. Every new session inside it starts from the same instructions and knowledge base, with no re-pasting required. That persistence is what makes a Gemini Gem a genuine home for a long campaign rather than a one-off chat.
