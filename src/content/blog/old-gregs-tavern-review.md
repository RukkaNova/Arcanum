Most AI dungeon masters have the personality of a hotel concierge: endlessly accommodating, professionally pleasant, and utterly forgettable. The category's big platforms sand their tone down to a friendly neutral because tone is risky and neutral ships. Old Greg's Tavern made the opposite bet — and the bet is the review.

## What It Is

Old Greg's Tavern is a persistent AI-run campaign platform on web, iOS, and Android. You build a character — custom appearance, class, backstory, stats — and Old Greg runs the table: a DM voice with actual bite, in a world that doesn't handle you gently. The site claims north of 285,000 players by mid-2026, which would make it one of the fastest-growing names in the AI DM space.

Structurally, it targets the exact market [Friends & Fables](/blog/friends-and-fables-review) built: long-session, continuing-campaign play, rather than the one-shot story fragments most AI toys produce. Campaigns persist across devices and sessions; you can put a story down mid-sentence and pick it up hours later.

## The Edge Is Real

Plenty of platforms *say* they're edgy. In practice that usually means a skull in the logo and the same beige narration underneath. Old Greg's actively tries to be edgy and — this is the surprising part — succeeds. The DM voice has attitude. The world has grit. NPCs are allowed to be unpleasant, situations are allowed to go badly, and the tone carries an irreverence that its competitors genuinely lack.

That sounds like a small thing. Over a fifty-round session, it is not. Tone is what you actually live inside during a long campaign, and a DM with a voice beats a DM with a thesaurus. If Friends & Fables has ever felt too well-behaved for your table, this is the correction — and it is the platform's single biggest reason to exist.

## Where It Sits: Between F&F and AI Dungeon

On the structure spectrum that defines this category, Old Greg's Tavern lands in a specific and honest place: it is less a game than Friends & Fables, but more a game than [AI Dungeon](/blog/ai-dungeon-review).

You get real characters with stats and enough campaign scaffolding to make progress feel earned — but less mechanical rigor than F&F brings. Fewer hard rules, less systematic depth, fewer moments where the system itself pushes back on you. The trade is speed and looseness: sessions move, and the platform never buries a good scene under bookkeeping.

It is also, frankly, a bit clunkier than its biggest competitor — interface rough edges, occasional seams in long sessions. Solid, but you will notice.

## The Best Group Deal in the Category

The pricing model hides the platform's cleverest feature: invited friends play free. Up to five people can join your game without paying anything; only the host spends credits. One subscription runs a whole table.

For group play, that quietly beats almost everything else in our [directory](/clients) — most platforms charge per seat or throttle guests. Here, the economics actively encourage you to fill the tavern.

The flip side: there is no meaningful free solo tier. Playing your own campaigns costs credits — $5 one-time for 5 credits (a 50-round story), $15/month for 200 rounds, or $25/month for 450 with discounted top-ups. As a guest you ride free; as a host you pay.

## Verdict: 4/5

Old Greg's Tavern walked into Friends & Fables' market and carved out a seat through sheer personality. Less rigorous, a bit clunkier — and the only AI DM we've tested whose voice we'd recognize blind. The persistence works, the friends-play-free model makes it the easy group pick, and the edge is not a marketing line. 4 out of 5.

## FAQ

**Is Old Greg's Tavern free to play?**
Only as a guest. Invited friends — up to five per game — play completely free on the host's credits. Playing your own campaigns requires credits: $5 one-time buys 5 credits (50 rounds), and subscriptions run $15/month for 200 rounds or $25/month for 450 rounds.

**Is Old Greg's Tavern better than Friends & Fables?**
Different, mostly. Friends & Fables has more mechanical rigor and structure; Old Greg's Tavern is looser, faster, and far edgier in tone. If you want systems that push back, pick Friends & Fables. If you want personality and grit in a long campaign, Old Greg's delivers something its competitors don't.

**What platforms does Old Greg's Tavern run on?**
Web, iOS, and Android. Campaigns persist across devices — you can pause mid-sentence on your phone and pick the story back up later at a desk.
