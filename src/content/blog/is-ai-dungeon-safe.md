## Is It Legit, or a Scam?

AI Dungeon is developed and operated by **Latitude**, a real company that has run the platform since 2019, when the game helped invent the AI-storytelling genre. This is not a fly-by-night operation: it has an established user base, official apps, and a public presence going back years.

The commercial side is ordinary and transparent. The free tier requires no payment details at all — no card, no trial that converts — so you can evaluate the platform with zero financial exposure. Paid subscriptions are standard recurring payments you can cancel anytime. Nothing about the business model is a trap.

Standard web hygiene still applies: sign in only on the official site and apps, and treat any lookalike domain or "free premium" link as hostile. That advice is generic for a reason — the platform itself is not the threat.

## Are Your Stories Private?

This is the question underneath the question, and it deserves a precise answer rather than a reassuring one.

**Private in the everyday sense: yes.** Unpublished single-player adventures are not visible to other players. Nobody browses your stories the way they browse the public scenario library. Publishing is an explicit, deliberate act.

**Private in the absolute sense: no.** Your text is generated and stored on the platform's servers and handled under Latitude's privacy policy — the same arrangement as every hosted AI platform, from [Janitor AI](/blog/is-janitor-ai-safe) to Character.AI. A hosted service can never offer the privacy of a notebook.

The rule that squares this circle is simple and worth adopting everywhere, not just here: **never put real personal information into roleplay.** No real full name, address, workplace, or financial details. Your stories are fiction; keep your real identity out of them. Do that, and the gap between "everyday private" and "absolutely private" stops mattering.

## The 2021 Controversy: What Actually Happened

No honest safety answer can skip this, so here it is plainly.

In 2021, it came out that **private user stories could be manually reviewed by human moderators** as part of content-moderation efforts tied to the filtering requirements of OpenAI, whose models powered AI Dungeon at the time. Players who believed their unpublished stories were theirs alone learned otherwise. The backlash was severe, it drove a wave of users to competitors, and it remains the single biggest scar on the platform's reputation. Long-time players still bring it up, and they are not wrong to.

What matters for a 2026 safety assessment is what changed afterwards, and the changes were structural rather than cosmetic:

- **The OpenAI dependency is gone.** AI Dungeon now runs on Latitude's own in-house models and other providers — so the third-party filtering mandate that drove the whole episode no longer exists.
- **The moderation approach was publicly overhauled.** Latitude's stated policy since 2022 is that unpublished single-player play is left alone — moderation energy goes to *published*, community-facing content instead, and human review is not a routine part of private play.

Should you trust it? That's genuinely personal. The fair summary is that the platform was caught doing something users hated, paid a real price, and rebuilt the specific machinery that caused it. If the incident permanently disqualifies AI Dungeon for you, that's a defensible position — the [AI Dungeon alternatives guide](/blog/ai-dungeon-alternatives) exists for exactly that reader. If you judge platforms on current architecture rather than history, the current architecture is fine.

## Content Safety and Filters

AI Dungeon's content posture is two-sided, and both sides are relevant to safety:

- **The default experience is filtered.** New accounts get a safe-mode experience, and mature content is an explicit, age-gated opt-in — not something the game springs on you.
- **The opt-in side is genuinely permissive.** For verified adult accounts, AI Dungeon is one of the more freedom-first mainstream platforms, which is a feature for its core audience and the reason it isn't a children's product.

That makes the age question easy to answer honestly: **not for young children.** App-store ratings place it in the older-teen-and-up bracket, the freeform text engine can wander even with filters on, and the unrestricted side is strictly for adults. It's designed for grown-ups who choose what kind of story they want — the wider landscape of that choice is mapped neutrally in our [uncensored AI roleplay guide](/blog/uncensored-ai-roleplay).

## Account and Payment Security

The unglamorous essentials, because your account is the asset actually worth protecting:

- **Use a strong, unique password.** If it leaks from anywhere, uniqueness limits the damage to one account.
- **Don't click "free premium" or "free credits" links** in comments or messages — that's the standard phishing route for any subscription platform.
- **Subscribe only through the official site or app stores.** Anyone offering discounted AI Dungeon subscriptions through back channels is selling you a compromised account or nothing.
- **Review your subscription tier occasionally.** The top tiers run $50–$100 a month; that's a real bill worth keeping deliberate. (Unsure what each tier buys? The [best AI Dungeon model guide](/blog/best-ai-dungeon-model) breaks the lineup down.)

## Is It Safe for Your Device?

Yes. AI Dungeon is a website with official mobile apps — not software from unknown sources — so there is no malware risk in the platform itself. The device-level risks live *around* popular platforms, not in them: fake "AI Dungeon" downloads, unofficial extensions, and lookalike sites. Get the app from the official app stores, play on the official website, install nothing else, and there is nothing here that threatens your computer or phone.
