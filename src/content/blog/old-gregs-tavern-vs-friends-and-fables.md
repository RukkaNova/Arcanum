**Old Greg's Tavern vs Friends & Fables** is a fight between two AI dungeon masters chasing the exact same market — long-session, continuing campaigns rather than one-off story fragments — with almost opposite personalities. Friends & Fables built the most mechanically rigorous AI tabletop available. Old Greg's Tavern looked at that same market and bet that tone, not rules, was the missing ingredient.

Both bets paid off, just for different players. This breaks down where they actually diverge — voice, mechanics, group economics, platform reach, and price — so you can tell which tavern is yours.

## What Each One Actually Is

[Old Greg's Tavern](/clients/old-gregs-tavern) is a persistent, cross-platform AI dungeon master. You build a character — appearance, class, backstory, stats — and Old Greg runs a continuing campaign that picks up exactly where you left it, whether that's on a phone mid-commute or a desktop that evening. By mid-2026 the site claims north of 285,000 players, one of the fastest-growing names in the category. Our [full Old Greg's Tavern review](/blog/old-gregs-tavern-review) rates it 4/5.

[Friends & Fables](/clients/friends-and-fables) is the current high-water mark for feature density in AI tabletop gaming. Its Game Master, Franz, routes each player turn through up to a dozen specialized models — one for lore, one for the character sheet, one for map coordinates, one for narrative — before assembling a response. That's the engineering behind a genuine ruleset rather than a description of one. Our [full Friends & Fables review](/blog/friends-and-fables-review) rates it 4.5/5, the highest-rated platform in our directory.

## Tone and Voice

This is the real fault line, and it's a bigger deal than it sounds. Most AI DM platforms — Friends & Fables included — sand their tone down to a friendly, competent neutral. It's a defensible design choice: neutral ships, neutral doesn't alienate anyone, neutral is safe.

Old Greg's Tavern refused that safety. The DM voice has actual attitude — grit, bite, irreverence baked into how the world responds to you, not layered on top as flavor text. NPCs are allowed to be unpleasant. Situations are allowed to go badly in ways that feel earned rather than arbitrary. Over a fifty-round session, a DM with a distinct voice reads completely differently from a DM with a thesaurus, and it's the single biggest reason players pick this platform over its more polished competitor.

Friends & Fables isn't toneless — its community has built everything from X-Men simulations to grimdark MorkBorg adaptations — but the baseline Franz voice stays professionally pleasant. If Friends & Fables has ever felt too well-behaved for your table, Old Greg's Tavern is the corrective.

## Mechanics and Structure

Here the position reverses. Friends & Fables runs a full V3 combat system: dynamic hex battlemaps, line-of-sight calculations, damage resistances and vulnerabilities, initiative tracking. A charisma check succeeding feels different from the AI simply agreeing with you, because there's a system adjudicating it.

Old Greg's Tavern sits in a deliberately looser middle ground — real character sheets and enough campaign scaffolding to make progress feel earned, but fewer hard rules and less systematic depth than Friends & Fables. On the structure spectrum that defines this category, it lands between Friends & Fables and freeform sandboxes like [AI Dungeon](/clients/ai-dungeon): more game than pure improv, less game than a full tactical VTT. Sessions move faster as a result, but players who want the system itself to push back hard will notice the gap.

## Multiplayer and Group Economics

Both platforms target group play, but the pricing models couldn't be more different. Friends & Fables charges by seat tier — $19.95/month unlocks four players (Starter), $39.95/month unlocks six (Legend) — so a full table means a real monthly commitment regardless of who's actually paying.

Old Greg's Tavern flips that model. Up to five invited friends play completely free on the host's credits; only the host spends anything. A single $15/month Hero subscription (200 rounds) can run a whole table for the cost of one player elsewhere. It's quietly one of the best group-play deals in the entire AI RPG directory — the tradeoff is that solo play is effectively pay-to-host, since there's no meaningful free solo tier.

## Platforms and Access

Old Greg's Tavern runs on web, iOS, and Android with full cross-device persistence — pause a session mid-sentence on your phone, resume at a desk hours later. Friends & Fables is a web-first product without the same native mobile parity, which matters if your group plays asynchronously across devices during the week.

## Price

Old Greg's Tavern uses a credit system: $5 one-time buys 5 credits (a 50-round story), $15/month gets 200 rounds, $25/month gets 450 rounds with discounted top-ups. Friends & Fables is a straight subscription ladder from Free (5–25 AI turns/day, 3 players) through $19.95 (Starter) to $39.95 (Legend), with two months free on annual billing.

For a casual group that mostly plays together, Old Greg's Tavern is dramatically cheaper. For a group that wants unlimited turns and the deepest character customization caps, Friends & Fables' higher price buys real additional capability, not just seats.
