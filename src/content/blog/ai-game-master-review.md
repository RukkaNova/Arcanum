Every platform we review gets the same final question: who should choose this over the alternatives? It's the question that turns a feature list into a verdict. For AI Game Master — Dungeon RPG, we spent a mobile-testing session looking for the answer and came back empty. This review is about why.

## What It Is

AI Game Master is a mobile text RPG for iOS and Android (a web version is listed as in development). Create a hero with stats, skills, and inventory, pick a genre from dark fantasy to sci-fi, and a GPT-driven game master narrates your freeform adventure, generating scene art along the way. Each action costs one token; free players get 5 tokens every 4 hours.

If that pitch sounds familiar, it should. It is the [AI Dungeon](/blog/ai-dungeon-review) pitch, nearly beat for beat: freeform narration, no enforced rules layer, standard context-window memory, metered actions.

## The Comparison It Can't Escape

Being an AI Dungeon-alike is not a crime — half the category descends from it. The problem is execution. Everywhere the two overlap, AI Game Master is the rougher version: rougher prose, rougher interface, less coherent long-session behavior, and the same memory drift with fewer tools to manage it. It resembles AI Dungeon in many ways; it feels like a lower-production-value edition of it in most of them.

At comparable subscription prices — $15 to $25 a month against AI Dungeon's tiers — the value question isn't close. And if you're willing to leave the freeform lane entirely, most of our [directory](/clients) offers more for the same money.

## The Storefront Tells You What to Expect

We try to review the game, not the website. But sometimes the website is data. At the time of this review, AI Game Master's own pricing page contained literal placeholder text — "Add terms and conditions" — alongside multiple misspellings ("Entuhsiast," "Unlimted," "Sequals") and two different tiers priced identically at $25 with wildly different token allowances.

Maybe it's unfinished; maybe it's unattended. Either way, when a paid product's storefront hasn't been proofread, it tells you how much polish to expect on the inside — and in our experience, the inside matched.

## Pricing

Free: 5 tokens every 4 hours, 5 story slots. Explorer: $15/month, 500 tokens. Enthusiast: $25/month, 1,000 tokens. Game Master: also $25/month, unlimited tokens and slots. (Yes — the two top tiers cost the same. We assume you'd pick the unlimited one.)

## Verdict: 1/5

We don't hand out 1-star ratings for being bad at something ambitious — we hand them out when we can't construct a reason to choose a platform over its obvious alternative. If you want freeform AI storytelling on your phone, [FableAI](/blog/fableai-review) does it with dramatically better production values, and AI Dungeon itself remains the more polished original. AI Game Master is the same idea, rougher, with a token meter. 1 out of 5.
