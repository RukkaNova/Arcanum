**Chub AI vs Janitor AI** is the comparison anyone deep enough into character-card roleplay eventually runs into — they're the two biggest platforms built around the same idea: a huge, permissive library of user-made personas you chat with directly, on a hosted model or your own. After first-hand time with both, the split is unusually clean: **they serve an identical use case, and Janitor AI simply executes it better.**

## The Short Version

| | Chub AI | Janitor AI |
|---|---|---|
| **What it is** | Character-card repository + chat platform | Character-chat platform with a huge library |
| **Card library** | Enormous, permissive, widely regarded as excellent | Enormous, hundreds of thousands of cards |
| **Platform polish** | Unpolished, unintuitive | More coherent, friendlier onboarding |
| **Hosted model** | Available, quality inconsistent | Janitor LLM — usable, clearly outclassed by BYOM |
| **Bring your own model** | Yes, via API/OpenRouter | Yes, via API/OpenRouter (well-documented) |
| **Setup guidance** | Minimal | Dedicated setup, safety, and DeepSeek guides |

## What Each Platform Actually Is

[Chub AI](/clients/chub-ai) — also known as Chub Venus — began as a character-card repository (the name is literally Character Hub) with a chat interface bolted on. That origin still shows: the catalogue is the product, and the ability to chat with a card directly on the site feels like an afterthought layered on top of a library.

[Janitor AI](/clients/janitor) is built the other way around — a chat-first character platform that happens to have an enormous library behind it. Users create and publish character cards, browse by popularity and tags, and start a conversation. It runs on its own in-house model, Janitor LLM, or on a model you bring yourself.

Structurally, they're doing the same job — card library plus chat surface, hosted models plus bring-your-own — which is exactly what makes them so directly comparable.

## Card Library: The Real Draw of Both

This is genuinely close. Chub AI's catalogue is somewhat more open by design and is widely regarded as one of the best character-card resources anywhere — a large share of its "characters" are really full scenarios or plots wearing a character card as a container, which is a genuine strength for anyone building deep worlds. Our [character card guide](/blog/how-to-write-a-character-card) points to Chub as where the format's power users congregate.

Janitor AI's library is also enormous — hundreds of thousands of cards spanning every genre and content rating — with the same caveat that applies everywhere in this space: popular doesn't reliably mean good, and reading a card's personality field and example dialogue before committing predicts session quality far better than its download count.

Since both platforms read the same widely-adopted card format, cards move between them (and to [SillyTavern](/blog/sillytavern-guide)) with little to no cleanup — so "which library is bigger" matters less in practice than which platform you actually want to *play* on.

## Platform Polish and Ease of Use

Here's where the comparison stops being close. Janitor AI has a more coherent, guided experience — a first-time user can find a card and start a decent conversation within minutes, and its rough spots (the weak in-house model, the BYOM setup) are well-documented with clear fixes in our [complete Janitor AI setup guide](/blog/how-to-use-janitor-ai).

Chub AI hands you a pile of cards, a list of models, and a settings page, with no onboarding and no guided path from "I found a card" to "I'm having a good session." It's the rare platform that's actually *harder* to get a good result from than the power-user tools it superficially resembles — it looks like a simple website, then quietly expects SillyTavern-level configuration knowledge to perform well.

## Model Options: Hosted vs Bring-Your-Own

Both platforms cover the same spectrum, which is genuinely uncommon — most competitors do only one. Chub AI offers hosted in-house models for instant play plus bring-your-own-model through an API key or OpenRouter. Janitor AI mirrors that shape: free Janitor LLM for zero-setup browsing, or BYOM for meaningfully better writing.

The difference isn't the *options* — it's the *quality and reliability* of the hosted experience. Chub AI's hosted model quality is inconsistent session to session depending on configuration a newcomer isn't guided through. Janitor AI's in-house model is honestly mediocre too, but the platform is upfront about BYOM being the real experience, and the path to set it up (including cheap options like [DeepSeek](/blog/janitor-ai-deepseek)) is clearly documented.

## Price

Both are free to browse and chat, with paid tiers unlocking stronger hosted models on each platform, and bring-your-own-model costing whatever your API provider charges on either. Price isn't the deciding factor here — the experience for that price is.

## Which Should You Choose?

**Choose Janitor AI if** you want a card platform that actually works as a destination — better onboarding, a more coherent chat experience, and clearer documentation for getting BYOM running well. **[Full Janitor AI review](/blog/janitor-ai)** (rated 4/5).

**Choose Chub AI if** you specifically want the most open, plot-forward card catalogue and don't mind doing your own configuration legwork — or if you're sourcing cards to run somewhere else entirely. **[Full Chub AI review](/blog/chub-ai-review)** (rated 2/5).

**Use both, honestly.** This is a common and sensible split: browse and source cards on Chub AI, since the catalogue is a real strength, then actually play them on Janitor AI — or on [SillyTavern](/blog/sillytavern-guide) if you want deeper control than either hosted platform offers. **[Full comparison: Chub AI vs SillyTavern](/blog/chub-ai-vs-sillytavern)**.

**Want a real game instead of a chat?** Neither platform tracks persistent world state or consequences. [RoleForge](/clients/roleforge) and [Friends & Fables](/clients/friends-and-fables) are built for that instead, and the free [Arcanum Originals](/arcanum-games) run structured campaigns on any frontier model you already pay for.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Chub AI or Janitor AI better?**
Janitor AI is the better overall platform — more coherent, friendlier for newcomers, and its rough edges are well-documented with known fixes. Chub AI's card library and model flexibility are genuinely excellent, but the platform around them is unpolished and unintuitive. Most people are better served treating Chub as a card source and Janitor (or SillyTavern) as where they actually play.

**Does Chub AI have a bigger character library than Janitor AI?**
Chub AI's catalogue is somewhat more open and is widely regarded as one of the largest character-card resources in the space, but Janitor AI's library is also enormous — hundreds of thousands of cards across every genre. Neither is a meaningful bottleneck; the difference that actually matters is how each platform helps (or fails to help) you find something good.

**Can I use Chub AI cards on Janitor AI?**
Largely yes. Both platforms use the widely-adopted community character-card format, so cards move between them with little to no cleanup. It's common practice to source a card on Chub AI and run it on Janitor AI, SillyTavern, or another card-compatible platform.

**Which is more permissive, Chub AI or Janitor AI?**
Both are explicitly more permissive than mainstream chat assistants, and the practical difference between them is small. Chub AI's catalogue is sometimes described as slightly more open at the margins, but the meaningful content-freedom decision for either platform is which model you connect, not which of the two sites you're on.

**Is Chub AI or Janitor AI easier to use?**
Janitor AI, clearly. It has a more coherent, guided experience for a first-time user. Chub AI looks like a simple website but quietly expects cockpit-level configuration knowledge — presets, model routing, settings — to get a good result, without explaining any of it up front.

**Should I use Chub AI, Janitor AI, or both?**
Using both is common and reasonable: browse and source cards on Chub AI, since its catalogue is a genuine strength, then run your favourites on Janitor AI (or SillyTavern) where the actual play experience is more polished. As a single destination, Janitor AI is the stronger pick.
