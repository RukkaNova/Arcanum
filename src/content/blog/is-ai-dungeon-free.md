**Yes, AI Dungeon is free to use.** There is no paywall to start playing, no credit card required to create an account, and no trial period that expires. You can sign up today and begin a story immediately at no cost.

What the free tier *gives you* is a more complicated question — and "unlimited" is a word in AI Dungeon's marketing that deserves a closer look before you take it at face value.

## What the Free Tier Actually Includes

The free tier runs on AI Dungeon's in-house finetunes — models like **Muse**, **Wayfarer Small 2**, and **Fable**, plus the **Dynamic Small** auto-switcher. They are genuinely capable of coherent, entertaining stories, and for a quick session, a one-shot adventure, or casual exploration of the format, they work well.

What you get for free:
- Full access to the story creation interface
- In-house model generation (Muse, Wayfarer, Fable, and more) with no hard session cap
- The scenario library (thousands of community-built starting points)
- Single-player adventures in every genre

The free experience improved significantly with AI Dungeon's Unchained update, which removed the energy system that previously rationed every generation. Free players are no longer interrupted mid-session by a prompt to wait or upgrade. You can play a full session without hitting a wall.

## What's Behind the Paywall

The paid tiers — Journey, Legend, Mythic, and Ultimate — unlock meaningful differences, not cosmetic ones.

**Model quality.** The premium plans unlock AI Dungeon's largest models — big finetunes like **Nova** and **Wayfarer Large** (70B) and top-tier options such as **DeepSeek V4 Pro** — with more nuance, stronger character consistency, and better coherence over long sessions than the smaller free finetunes. If you have been playing on the free tier and found the writing repetitive or the story logic weak, the model is often the reason. Our [best AI Dungeon model guide](/blog/best-ai-dungeon-model) breaks down the current lineup and which to pick for your playstyle.

**Memory and context.** Premium tiers get extended memory options, which directly affects how well AI Dungeon holds your story together over long campaigns. The free tier's memory handling is functional for short sessions and degrades over long ones.

**Multiplayer.** Shared adventures, where you play with another person in the same story, are a paid feature. The free tier is single-player only.

**Adventure sharing and publishing.** Creating public scenarios for other players to use requires a paid subscription.

**Priority access.** During high-traffic periods, free users may experience slower generation. Paid tiers get priority in the queue.

## Is "Unlimited" Actually Unlimited?

This is the question that deserves a direct answer, because the word "unlimited" appears in AI Dungeon's paid tier marketing and means something specific — not what most people assume.

**"Unlimited" on paid plans means there is no hard credit cap.** On the free tier, AI Dungeon does not currently impose a daily credit limit that hard-stops your session (this changed with the Unchained update). On paid plans, there is similarly no cap on the number of generations you can request.

What "unlimited" does *not* mean: you can generate as fast as you want, indefinitely, with no throttling. Under heavy sustained use — long sessions, rapid inputs, extended campaigns over many hours — AI Dungeon applies rate limiting. This is standard practice for any AI platform offering generative output at scale, and AI Dungeon is no exception. Players who try to run marathon sessions or use the platform intensively will encounter slowdowns regardless of which tier they are on.

The practical answer for most players: the limits on paid tiers are high enough that you will not notice them in normal play. Casual to moderate users will not hit the ceiling. Players running extended daily campaigns or power-using the platform may.

The free tier is similarly unrestricted in terms of hard caps but is constrained in a different way: the smaller free models and their tighter context windows make very long campaigns frustrating before generation limits become the problem.

## Who the Free Tier Is and Isn't For

**The free tier is good for:**
- Players who have never tried AI-driven interactive fiction and want to see what it is
- Casual one-off sessions and genre experiments
- Testing the interface before deciding whether to pay
- Players who want freeform creative freedom and aren't concerned with long-term campaign coherence

**The free tier is not adequate for:**
- Long campaigns where character and world consistency matter
- Players who have experienced frontier model quality (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini) and will notice the difference
- Anything requiring extended memory, multiplayer, or reliable narrative persistence
