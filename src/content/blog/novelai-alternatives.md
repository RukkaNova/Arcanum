**NovelAI is the best AI writing studio most people will ever use — and that's exactly why some of them go looking for something else.** Made by Anlatan, it hands you an editor, fiction-tuned prose that reads like a novel, and a Lorebook that keeps your world's detail in play across a long manuscript. If your goal is to *write* a story, little else comes close, as our [full NovelAI review](/blog/novelai-review) lays out.

But a writing studio is not a game. NovelAI asks you to author and steer every beat yourself; it won't reason about your choices, enforce a ruleset, or keep a world consistent on its own. Pair that with no genuinely usable free tier, and you have the three reasons people search for an alternative: **the price, the fact that it's a studio rather than a game, and the reasoning gaps that show up over a long session.** The right alternative depends on which of those is your dealbreaker.

---

## Why the Right Alternative Depends on What You Want Instead

People leave NovelAI for different reasons, and each points to a different tool:

- **The price** — there's no real free tier, only a limited trial, so you're paying to play before you know if it fits.
- **It's a studio, not a game** — you wanted an AI to *run* a world for you, not an editor you drive sentence by sentence.
- **Reasoning and consistency** — over a long story it drifts into plot holes and contradicts what it set up an hour ago.
- **No live knowledge** — it doesn't browse, so it misses anything outside its training for grounded or reference-heavy play.
- **You still want the prose** — and you're hoping to keep novel-grade writing while fixing one of the above.

The alternatives below are sorted by which of those they actually solve. For why long AI stories tend to fall apart on *every* platform — NovelAI included — our guide on [why LLMs forget](/blog/why-llms-forget) covers the underlying memory mechanics.

## 1. Arcanum Originals — Best for a Game That Actually Reasons (Free)

If what NovelAI lacked was a *game* — something that reasons about consequences, enforces rules, and runs a world instead of waiting for you to write it — this is the most direct fix, and it costs nothing.

The [Arcanum Originals](/arcanum-games) are engineered RPG systems you download and load into a frontier model you may already pay for — ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini — as a Custom GPT, Project, or Gem. The flagship, **[Aevum Realm Architect](/arcanum-games/aevum)**, is a deterministic kingdom-builder: you rise from serf to monarch through economy, politics, and warfare, with a Deference Engine that enforces social hierarchy, a hidden GM notepad tracking every asset and NPC, and a `/fix_state` command to correct drift. Crucially, the *reasoning* is whatever frontier model you bring — so the consistency and logic gaps that hold NovelAI back simply aren't your engine's problem. [Read the Aevum review](/blog/aevum-realm-architect) or [download it free](/arcanum-games/aevum).

**Solves:** wanting a real game, reasoning, structure, price
**Cost:** free (requires ChatGPT Plus, Claude Pro, or Gemini Advanced for knowledge-file features)

---

## 2. AI Dungeon — Best for Playing a Story With a Real Free Tier

If your instinct is "I don't want to *write* a story, I want to *play* one" — and you don't want to pay just to find out whether you like it — **[AI Dungeon](/blog/ai-dungeon-review)** is the closest like-for-like swap. Where NovelAI is an editor, AI Dungeon is an adventure: it prompts you with a situation and responds to what you do, which is a fundamentally more game-like loop. And unlike NovelAI, its free tier is genuinely playable, so the price barrier disappears.

The trade-offs run the other way — AI Dungeon's prose is more generic than NovelAI's fiction-tuned output, and its filter and memory have well-documented limits. We put the two head to head, beat by beat, in [AI Dungeon vs NovelAI](/blog/ai-dungeon-vs-novelai).

**Solves:** wanting a game not a studio, price
**Cost:** free tier available, paid tiers for heavier use

## 3. SillyTavern — Best for Worldbuilding Control Without the Subscription

If what you loved about NovelAI was the **Lorebook** — building a world and having the AI pull in its detail on demand — but you resented paying for a model you didn't choose, **[SillyTavern](/blog/sillytavern-guide)** is the power-user answer. It's a free, open-source front-end with its own World Info lorebook system, and it's model-agnostic: you connect whatever you want underneath, from a budget API to a frontier model to a fully private local model on your own hardware.

That single fact fixes two NovelAI complaints at once. The reasoning is as strong as the model you connect (so you can route through a top-tier reasoning model), and the cost is whatever that model charges — free if you run local. The catch is setup: SillyTavern is a cockpit, not a turnkey app. Which model to pick is its own decision — see [best LLM for roleplay](/blog/best-llm-for-roleplay).

**Solves:** price, reasoning (bring a stronger model), keeping worldbuilding control, privacy
**Cost:** free (model costs separate; free if local)

---

## 4. RoleForge — Best for a Story That Stays Consistent

The NovelAI weakness that stings most over a long campaign is consistency — the plot holes and contradictions that accumulate because the model is a writer, not a database. **[RoleForge](/clients/roleforge)** is built around the opposite priority: a structured, database-backed approach to characters and world state, designed to keep a long story coherent rather than letting it drift. If you kept hitting "wait, didn't that character die two chapters ago?" in NovelAI, this is the targeted fix.

**Solves:** consistency, plot holes, long-campaign coherence
**Cost:** freemium

---

## 5. Friends & Fables — Best for Structured Mechanics and Multiplayer

If the deeper truth is that you never really wanted a *writing* tool at all — you wanted a tabletop RPG with rules, dice, and a game master — **[Friends & Fables](/clients/friends-and-fables)** is the most structured LLM roleplay platform available. It runs a real system: stats, dice, combat, companions, and an AI game master that maintains session state, plus multiplayer for up to six players. Where NovelAI hands you a blank editor, Friends & Fables hands you a game with a framework and outcomes that actually mean something.

The trade-off is the inverse of NovelAI's: structure is also a constraint, and meaningful play requires a subscription.

**Solves:** wanting mechanics and structure, multiplayer
**Cost:** freemium, paid subscription for extended play

---

## 6. DreamGen — Best for Narrative Freedom on Open Models

For the player whose NovelAI itch was narrative writing without a subscription wall, **DreamGen** is the closest writing-focused alternative on this list. It runs open-weight models tuned for storytelling, with a scenario codex where you define plot, setting, style, and characters upfront so the AI threads them through every scene — conceptually similar to leaning on a Lorebook. It has a free tier and handles multi-character narrative scenes well. The interface is rougher than NovelAI's polished editor, and the prose, while strong, isn't quite NovelAI's fiction-tuned class — but the free entry point makes it easy to try.

**Solves:** price, narrative writing freedom
**Cost:** free tier available, paid tiers for heavier use

## What NovelAI Is Still Best At (An Honest Note)

It's worth being straight about this: if your only priority is **prose quality**, NovelAI is still the best tool in this whole space, and most of the alternatives above don't beat it on that one axis — they beat it on price, mechanics, reasoning, or structure. NovelAI's models are fine-tuned on fiction in a way that reads like a novel rather than an assistant, and nothing here matches that out of the box.

So the honest framing isn't "NovelAI is bad." It's that NovelAI is a **writing studio**, and if what you actually want is a *game* — a world that reasons, remembers, and holds together on its own — you were always slightly using the wrong tool. The alternatives above are the right tools for that job. (And if you keep NovelAI for prose but want better long-story consistency from any tool, the free [Campaign Memory Tool](/tools/campaign-memory) tracks your canonical world state alongside whatever you write in.)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best alternative to NovelAI?**
It depends on why you're leaving. If you want an actual game that reasons about your choices rather than a writing studio you steer by hand, an engineered system like Arcanum's [Aevum](/arcanum-games/aevum) running on a frontier model is the strongest option. If you want to play a story with a genuinely free tier, [AI Dungeon](/blog/ai-dungeon-review) is the closest like-for-like. If you want worldbuilding control without the subscription, [SillyTavern](/blog/sillytavern-guide) paired with a model of your choice is the power-user pick.

**Is there a free alternative to NovelAI?**
Yes. NovelAI has no genuinely usable free tier, which is the most common reason people look elsewhere. AI Dungeon has a playable free tier, SillyTavern is free and open-source if you bring your own model, and the [Arcanum Originals](/arcanum-games) are free downloads that run inside a frontier model you may already pay for.

**What is better than NovelAI for prose quality?**
For pure prose, NovelAI is still hard to beat — its models are fine-tuned on fiction and read like a novel rather than an assistant. The honest answer is that most alternatives win on game mechanics, reasoning, or price, not on prose. If prose is your only priority, the main alternative is connecting a strong frontier model through a front-end like SillyTavern.

**Why does NovelAI create plot holes in long stories?**
NovelAI's models are tuned for prose rather than reasoning, so over a long session they drift into inconsistency and contradict things established earlier. Its Lorebook helps store world facts, but the model's handling of them isn't airtight. Platforms built around a structured database, or stronger reasoning models, hold a long story together more reliably.

**Is NovelAI good for roleplay or just writing?**
NovelAI is built for writing — an editor where you author and steer every beat. It can be used for roleplay, but it doesn't run a game for you the way a dedicated RPG platform does: it won't reason about consequences, enforce rules, or keep a world consistent on its own. For roleplay where the AI runs the world, a game-first tool serves you better.

**Do I need to pay for a NovelAI alternative?**
Not necessarily. Several strong alternatives are free: AI Dungeon's free tier is playable, SillyTavern is free and open-source, and the Arcanum Originals are free downloads. Others, like Friends & Fables or RoleForge, are subscription platforms — so you can match the cost to whether you want free play or a fuller managed experience.
