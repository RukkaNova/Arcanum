Dunia is an AI storytelling platform built around a simple pitch: you create a world, the AI generates its lore, characters, and locations, then narrates and games-masters while you play inside it. It's less interested in simulating D&D-style mechanics than in producing high-quality collaborative fiction — a narrator/GM that keeps NPCs consistent and prose genuinely good, rather than a rules engine that tracks hit points.

## How It Works

World creation is fast: describe a premise and Dunia fills in the lore, cast, and locations for you to start playing in immediately. From there it acts as narrator and GM, but with an author's toolkit layered on top that pure "game" platforms don't have — you can edit any previous message and branch the story into alternate timelines, exploring a different path without losing the one you already played.

## Where It Sits

On the spectrum between sandbox and system, Dunia lands in the middle: less freeform than [AI Dungeon](/clients/ai-dungeon), less mechanically rigorous than [Friends & Fables](/clients/friends-and-fables). It isn't trying to be either — it's closer to an interactive novel-writing experience than a game with stats and progression, and it's honest about that trade rather than pretending to be a rules system it isn't.

## What It Does Best (and Worst)

Best at prose quality and NPC consistency — characters hold their personalities better than most competitors across a session, and the writing is a cut above the category average. World creation is genuinely easy, and the branching-timeline system is a real, useful feature rather than a marketing line: exploring "what if I'd done that instead" without losing your original thread is rare in this space.

Worst for anyone who wants a game system. There's little mechanical progression, no stat-based advancement, and no tactical combat — fights resolve narratively, not on a grid. If you came for D&D-style rulesets, look elsewhere.

## Pricing

Freemium, credit-based, with genuinely unlimited world creation and session slots at every tier — only the daily credit allowance changes.

| Plan | Cost | Credits/Day | Rollover |
|------|------|-------------|----------|
| Free | $0 | 75/day | None |
| Imagineer | $19.95/mo | 200/day | Up to 3 days (cap 600) |
| Myth Maker | $39.95/mo | 400/day | Up to 6 days (cap 2400) |

One-time credit packs are also available (300 for $3, up to 8,910 for $81 at a 10% bonus) for occasional players who'd rather top up than subscribe. Only paid tiers count toward Dunia's creator revenue share — see below.

## Creators Earn Too

Dunia pays its world-builders: publish a world, and you earn 20% of what other players spend inside it. It's a different model from [Hidden Door](/clients/hidden-door)'s licensed-IP creator payouts — this is a general community revenue share rather than rights-holder partnerships — but it's a genuine reason for a creator with an audience to build here rather than elsewhere.

## Verdict

Dunia sits precisely where it claims to: not as much a game as Friends & Fables, not as much a sandbox as AI Dungeon. For collaborative storytelling and worldbuilding — where prose quality, consistent characters, and the freedom to edit and branch your story matter more than a ruleset — it's a genuinely great experience. For anyone wanting D&D and real mechanics, it isn't the platform, since combat and progression stay narrative throughout. 4 out of 5.
