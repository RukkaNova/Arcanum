Old Greg's Tavern is a text-based AI RPG built around a simple pitch: a dungeon master who is always awake and never polite about it. You create a character — custom appearance, class, backstory, stats — walk into the tavern, and the campaign runs whenever you do: web, iOS, or Android, pick up mid-sentence and put it down hours later. The site claims over 285,000 players, and by mid-2026 it has been one of the fastest-growing names in the AI DM space.

## How It Works

Structurally, Old Greg's Tavern sits in the same territory as [Friends & Fables](/clients/friends-and-fables): persistent, long-session campaign play with real character sheets, aimed at people who want a continuing world rather than one-off story fragments. Sessions run on a credit system — one story of up to 50 rounds costs 5 credits — and the standout multiplayer hook is that invited friends (up to five) play completely free; only the host spends credits.

Where it splits from Friends & Fables is temperament. This platform actively tries to be edgy, and it succeeds — there is a genuine edge to the DM voice and the world's attitude that its competitors simply lack. Grit, bite, and irreverence are the house style, not an accident of the model.

## Advantages Over Competitors

The edginess is the real differentiator. Most AI DM platforms sand their tone down to a friendly neutral; Old Greg's commits to a voice, and long-session players who find the competition bland will feel the difference immediately. On the spectrum that matters in this category, it is less a game than Friends & Fables but more a game than [AI Dungeon](/clients/ai-dungeon) — enough structure to feel like a campaign, enough looseness to stay fast.

The friends-play-free model is also quietly one of the best group-play deals in the directory: one subscriber can host a whole table.

## What It Does Best (and Worst)

Best for long-session, personality-forward campaign play — solo or with friends — where tone matters as much as mechanics. If Friends & Fables ever felt too well-behaved for your table, this is the correction.

Worst for players who want mechanical rigor. It has less of it than Friends & Fables — fewer hard rules, less systematic depth — and the experience is a bit clunkier overall. Solid, but you will notice the rough edges in the interface and the occasional seams in long sessions.

## Limitations

The credit economy means solo play is effectively pay-to-host: there is no meaningful free solo tier, so budget for a subscription if you intend to play regularly rather than join friends' games. And the mechanical looseness that keeps sessions fast also caps the depth — this is not the platform for players who want the system itself to push back hard.

## Verdict

Old Greg's Tavern targets the same long-session market as Friends & Fables and carves out its own seat at the table through sheer personality. A bit clunkier, less mechanically rigorous — but the edge is real, the persistence works, and the friends-play-free model makes it an easy group pick. 4 out of 5.
