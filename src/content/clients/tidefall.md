Tidefall is a D&D-inspired "living world" AI RPG, and it announces its intentions from the opening prologue: looping background animations, rich hand-drawn art, and a soundscape that all pull in the same direction. It reads as carefully designed rather than generated on the fly, closer in spirit to [Fable Forge](/blog/fable-forge-first-look) than to the freewrite sandboxes that dominate this category — both platforms are willing to narrow what's possible in exchange for a more consistent, more immersive world.

That trade shows in the writing and presentation. It also currently shows in the UX, which open beta hasn't caught up to yet: no inventory screen, no party screen, no quest journal. You track a lot of state by memory and guesswork instead.

We haven't rated Tidefall yet. See our [first look](/blog/tidefall-first-look) for the fuller take on those first 24 hours — a full rated review will follow once the beta matures.
