NovelAI, built by Anlatan, is the odd one out in this directory: it isn't really trying to be a game. It's an author's studio — a tool for *writing* fiction with an AI co-writer rather than *playing* a session. Launched in 2021 with a hard focus on prose and privacy, it carved out a loyal following among writers, and on the things it set out to do it's still excellent. As an AI RPG, though, it's been overtaken.

## How It Works

Instead of a chat or an adventure prompt, NovelAI gives you an editor. You write, the model continues, and you steer — rewriting, retrying, and nudging the direction as you go. Its models are tuned specifically on fiction rather than on answering questions, which is the whole point and the source of its biggest advantage. The standout structural feature is the **Lorebook**: a manual memory system where you pin characters, factions, locations, and world rules so the model keeps recalling them across a long manuscript. Image generation is bundled in as well, but text is the heart of it.

## What It Does Best

The prose is the headline. Most alternatives run on models trained to answer questions, and it shows — their output reads like an assistant describing a scene. NovelAI reads like a novel. For anyone who cares about the actual sentences, that gap is real and immediately felt.

The Lorebook is the other genuine strength. Treated as a worldbuilding tool rather than just a memory patch, it's a pleasure to use: a place to keep all the flavourful detail of a setting and have the AI actually draw on it. If you're building a world and a long-form story, the collaborative and novel-writing features are the best-in-class reason to be here.

## Where It Falls Behind

For playing a *game*, NovelAI no longer leads the pack, and the reasons are consistent. Its reasoning is weaker than the current alternatives — it's a writer, not a thinker, and complex situations expose that. It doesn't browse the internet as it answers, so it misses knowledge a connected model would have. And in longer play it creates plot holes with some regularity and drifts into inconsistency — the Lorebook helps you *store* lore, but the model's handling of it isn't airtight.

The net effect: the things that make NovelAI a superb writing studio (fiction-tuned prose, manual lore) don't add up to a strong game engine, where reasoning, consistency, and current knowledge matter more.

## Pricing

There's no genuinely usable free tier — only a limited trial — so meaningful use means a subscription.

| Plan | Monthly Cost | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Paper | Free (trial) | Limited generations, evaluation only |
| Tablet | ~$10 | Entry subscription, smaller context |
| Scroll | ~$15 | Larger context |
| Opus | ~$25 | Best model access, largest context, unlimited lower-model generations |

The flat pricing is cheaper than the top tiers of some rivals, but the absence of a real free tier raises the barrier to trying it.

## Verdict

NovelAI is the best tool here if your goal is to *write* — novel-grade prose and a Lorebook that doubles as a worldbuilder make it a joy for long-form fiction. As an AI RPG it's slipped behind, held back by weaker reasoning, no live knowledge, and a tendency toward plot holes and inconsistency in extended play. Come for the writing studio, not the game.
